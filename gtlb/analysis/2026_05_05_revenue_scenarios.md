## Scenario Build-Up (FY2027 Guidance vs Scenarios)

### FY2027 Guidance (Provided by Management)
- **Midpoint:** $1.109B (15-17% growth)
- **Conservative positioning:** Acknowledges bookings growth lag, ratable model mechanics, non-recurring FY26 tailwinds (~300 bps)
- **Embedded prudence:** First order team ramping (back half impact), DAP minimal contribution, PubSec/mid-market caution

### Three-Scenario Build-Up

**BASE CASE = $1.108B (+15.5% YoY)**
- Reflects management guidance midpoint with slight upside
- First order inflection materializes (evidenced by Q4 logos growth)
- Sales capacity fully operational by Q3, drives net new ARR improvement
- Price-sensitive cohort stabilizes at elevated levels (not recovering quickly)
- DAP contributes <$10M (pilots converting, but mostly FY28 recognition)
- SaaS mix reaches 35% (up from 32%), growing at higher rate (35-40% growth)
- Assumes successful execution on 5 growth initiatives

**BULL CASE = $1.180M (+23.5% YoY)**
- First orders accelerate faster than expected (new product-led on-ramps, sales team effectiveness)
- DAP adoption begins earlier in year (customer urgency on AI/agents higher)
- Public sector shows partial recovery (budget clarity post-government reopening)
- Price-sensitive cohort stabilizes faster (technical services + DAP credits drive engagement)
- Enterprise/Ultimate tier acceleration (security spending, AI driver)
- SaaS/Dedicated mix reaches 40%+, growing at 40%+ (higher margin stream)
- NRR recovers toward 120% as expansion story takes hold
- Territory design and sales enablement exceed internal projections

**BEAR CASE = $1.055B (+10.4% YoY)**
- Macro headwinds persist (enterprise budget constraints, recession fears)
- First order inflection fails to materialize (pipeline doesn't convert at expected rate)
- DAP adoption significantly slower (technical requirements for self-managed, market education gap)
- Price-sensitive cohort continues secular decline (-8-10% annually)
- Mid-market weakness spreads (technology consolidation, cost-cutting)
- Public sector remains challenged (government funding uncertainty)
- NRR continues to compress (115% → 110%)
- SaaS mix growth underperforms (customers prefer capital efficiency over OpEx)

## Key Drivers & Assumptions by Scenario

### Driver 1: First Order Acceleration
| Scenario | Driver | Impact | Confidence |
|----------|--------|--------|-----------|
| **Bull** | New sales team + product-led initiatives drive 20%+ new logo growth YoY | +$80-100M bookings incremental to base | Medium-High |
| **Base** | Sales team ramps to 80% productivity, modest new logo acceleration (+8-12%) | +$30-40M bookings incremental | High |
| **Bear** | Sales team underperforms (70% productivity), new logo growth stalls (0-2%) | -$20-30M bookings vs plan | Medium |

*Evidence:* Q4 saw "most $1M+ customers in history" and 4 consecutive months of improved first order logos (Oct-Jan inflection). However, Q4 also saw U.S. softness and deal slippage. Management action (dedicated first order team with 4 regional leads, rapid hiring) is clear. Team has already closed first deals in Q1.

### Driver 2: DAP (Duo Agent Platform) Adoption
| Scenario | Driver | FY27 Contribution | FY28 Contribution | Confidence |
|----------|--------|-------------------|-------------------|-----------|
| **Bull** | Early adoption success, customer enthusiasm for AI automation drives rapid trials → production conversion by H2 FY27 | $15-20M | $150-200M | Medium |
| **Base** | Measured adoption curve (70% self-managed needs upgrade to 18.8+), pilots convert late FY27/FY28 | $5-10M | $80-100M | High |
| **Bear** | Slower adoption, integration friction, competitive agent pressure, customer hesitation | $0-5M | $30-50M | Medium |

*Evidence:* DAP launched mid-January (7 weeks into FY27). Management explicitly states "minimal revenue contribution FY27, meaningful FY28+". Early customer examples (airline 90% automation, insurance hackathon) are strong. However, 70% self-managed deployment means 6-month upgrade curve (50% adoption = 2 quarters). Committed credits model means revenue recognition lags contracts by 1-2 quarters.

### Driver 3: Sales Capacity & Territory Design Ramp
| Scenario | Driver | Impact | Timeline |
|----------|--------|--------|----------|
| **Bull** | Territory redesign + new leadership (Ian + Manav) drives faster than expected ramp, 85%+ productivity by Q3 | +$50-60M incremental revenue | Q3-Q4 visibility |
| **Base** | Planned ramp: 75-80% productivity by Q3, full productivity Q4 | +$25-35M incremental revenue | Q3-Q4 payoff |
| **Bear** | Ramp delays, higher churn, productivity plateaus at 65-70% | +$10-15M incremental revenue | Q4+ impact only |

*Evidence:* Management stated "entering FY27 with more capacity than ever" and "path to stronger ramped capacity beginning Q3". But territory overhaul reduces customers per rep (better coverage but lower initial productivity). Investment is real, payoff timeline is clear.

### Driver 4: Price-Sensitive Cohort Stabilization (~20% of ARR)
| Scenario | Driver | Impact on NRR | Intervention |
|----------|--------|----------------|--------------|
| **Bull** | DAP credits + lower-tier offerings + technical services drive rapid stickiness improvement | NRR recovers 120%+ | All initiatives work together |
| **Base** | Gradual stabilization, some churn continues but slows | NRR stabilizes 115-117% | Credits + coverage + adoption support |
| **Bear** | Continued churn, initiatives insufficient to reverse trend | NRR declines to 110% | Pricing power lost in segment |

*Evidence:* Management identified ~20% of ARR as price-sensitive (SMB + parts of mid-market/Premium). Q4 saw softer U.S. performance and budget constraints. Management response: DAP credits with every Premium seat, adjusted coverage models, technical services investment. These are credible mitigants but execution-dependent.

### Driver 5: SaaS Mix Expansion
| Scenario | Driver | FY27 Target | FY28 Target |
|----------|--------|------------|------------|
| **Bull** | GitLab Dedicated traction + DAP hosted adoption + Duo + security features | 40%+ of revenue | 45%+ |
| **Base** | Steady SaaS/Dedicated growth (38-40% YoY), mix reaches 35-38% | 35-38% | 38-42% |
| **Bear** | SaaS growth meets overall growth (30-35% YoY), mix reaches 32-35% only | 32-35% | 34-38% |

*Evidence:* FY26 Q4: SaaS was 32% of revenue, growing 38% YoY. Management expects margin compression from SaaS mix (higher COGS for cloud-hosted). This is intentional strategic shift. Bull case assumes faster adoption of Dedicated and hosted DAP.

## Summary

This revenue model provides three internally consistent paths for GitLab's next three years:

- **Bull Case** ($1.18B FY27, +24%) requires successful execution on ALL five growth initiatives plus faster DAP adoption
- **Base Case** ($1.11B FY27, +16%) assumes execution on planned initiatives with conservative DAP timing
- **Bear Case** ($1.06B FY27, +10%) assumes execution shortfalls and prolonged macro headwinds

The **Base Case is management-guided** and represents the midpoint of official guidance with credible support from Q4 momentum (highest net new ARR ever, first order inflection). The **Bull and Bear cases bracket realistic outcomes** depending on execution and external factors.

**Probability-weighted price target of $36-51** implies +44-104% upside from current $25 price, with asymmetric risk/reward favoring long investors with 3+ year horizon.
