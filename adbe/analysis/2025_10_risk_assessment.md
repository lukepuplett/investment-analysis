## Executive Summary

Adobe faces several key risks across competitive, technological, regulatory, and financial dimensions. While the company has strong competitive advantages and financial health, intensifying competition in AI, evolving regulatory landscape, and technology disruption risks require careful monitoring. The company's AI-first strategy, while successful, also introduces new risks related to AI regulation, IP concerns, and technology dependencies.

## Risk Assessment Framework

### Risk Categories
- **Competitive Risks**: Market competition and market share erosion
- **Technology Risks**: AI performance, obsolescence, and disruption
- **Regulatory Risks**: AI regulation, data privacy, and compliance
- **Financial Risks**: Debt levels, cash flow, and economic sensitivity
- **Operational Risks**: Talent retention, execution, and scaling

### Risk Severity Levels
- **High**: Significant impact on business, requires immediate attention
- **Medium**: Moderate impact, requires monitoring and mitigation
- **Low**: Limited impact, standard business risk

## High-Risk Factors

### 1. AI Competition from Tech Giants
**Risk Level**: High
**Description**: Intensifying competition from Google, Microsoft, and Meta in AI creative tools
**Impact**: Potential market share erosion in AI-first products
**Probability**: High
**Mitigation**:
- Focus on workflow integration and enterprise features
- Maintain technology leadership through innovation
- Strengthen ecosystem lock-in through platform integration
- Continue strategic partnerships and acquisitions

### 2. AI Regulation and IP Concerns
**Risk Level**: High

### 3. Technology Disruption
