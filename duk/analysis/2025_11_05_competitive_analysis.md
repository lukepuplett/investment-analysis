## Executive Summary

**BLUF: Duke Energy maintains dominant market positions as a regulated utility monopoly across six Southeast and Midwest states, serving 8.6M electric and 1.7M gas customers. Competitive advantages include the largest T&D system in the U.S. (~320K miles), supportive regulatory constructs enabling efficient capital recovery, strong economic development track record (AWS $10B investment), and execution capability on large-scale projects. The company faces minimal direct competition due to regulated monopoly status but must compete for economic development projects. Strategic asset transactions strengthen competitive positioning while enabling capital investment. COMPETITIVE POSITION: STRONG.**

## Competitive Threats and Challenges

### 1. Distributed Generation

**Threat Level: Moderate**

**Challenges:**
- **Residential Solar**: Customers installing rooftop solar reduces retail sales
- **Battery Storage**: Energy storage enables greater self-generation
- **Cost Declines**: Declining solar/battery costs increase adoption

**Mitigating Factors:**
- **Decoupling Mechanisms**: Residential decoupling reduces volumetric exposure (~20% of volumes)
- **Integration Services**: Utility can provide grid services for distributed resources
- **Regulatory Support**: States maintain utility role in grid management

### 2. Energy Efficiency

**Threat Level: Low-Moderate**

**Challenges:**
- **Efficiency Programs**: State-mandated efficiency programs reduce demand
- **Technology**: LED lighting, efficient appliances reduce consumption

**Mitigating Factors:**
- **Rate Recovery**: Efficiency program costs typically recoverable
- **Load Growth**: Strong load growth offsets efficiency impacts
- **Customer Growth**: Population growth offsets per-customer efficiency

### 3. Regulatory Changes

**Threat Level: Low**

**Potential Challenges:**
- **Rate Case Outcomes**: Adverse rate case decisions could impact returns
- **Policy Changes**: Shifts in regulatory policy could affect recovery mechanisms
- **Political Changes**: Changes in state leadership could affect regulatory support

**Mitigating Factors:**
- **Diversified Jurisdictions**: Multiple states reduce single-jurisdiction risk
- **Constructive Relationships**: Strong regulatory relationships across jurisdictions
- **Supportive Legislation**: Recent legislation enhances regulatory framework

### 4. Economic Development Competition

**Threat Level: Low**

**Competitive Dynamics:**
- **Site Selection**: Utilities compete for large industrial projects
- **Power Costs**: Competitive power pricing important for project selection
- **Infrastructure**: Pre-positioned infrastructure provides advantage

**Competitive Advantages:**
- **Site Readiness Program**: Proactive infrastructure development
- **Execution Track Record**: Proven ability to deliver on commitments
- **Partnership Approach**: Collaborative relationships with economic development teams
