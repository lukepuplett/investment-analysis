## Regulatory Environment

### State-Level Regulatory Framework

**North Carolina:**
- **Recent Legislation**: Power Bill Reduction Act (SB 266)
  - Allows annual recovery of financing costs for new baseload generation
  - Supports credit profile and minimizes customer costs
  - Multi-year rate plan structure
- **Status**: NC ranked #1 for business environment
- **Regulatory Construct**: Multi-year rate plans, grid modernization riders, renewables riders

**South Carolina:**
- **Recent Legislation**: Energy Security Act (H.3309)
  - Supports all-of-the-above strategy
  - Rate stabilization mechanism (similar to gas utilities)
  - Annual rate true-ups reduce volatility
- **Status**: Strong regulatory support
- **Active Rate Cases**: DEC and DEP rate cases filed, hearings in Q4 2025

**Florida:**
- **Regulatory Construct**: Multi-year rate plan
- **Recent Activity**: DEF capital plan increased by $4B
- **Brookfield Investment**: $6B minority investment validates attractive jurisdiction

**Ohio:**
- **Recent Legislation**: House Bill 15
  - Replaces Electric Security Plan with multi-year forward-looking rate making
  - Reduces regulatory lag
  - Multi-year rate plans available as of August 2025

**Indiana:**
- **Regulatory Support**: Constructive regulatory outcomes
- **Generation Investments**: Cayuga combined cycle projects advancing

### Federal Regulatory Support

**Nuclear Production Tax Credits:**
- **Preservation**: Extended through 2032 in budget reconciliation bill
- **Benefit**: $500M in PTCs earned in 2024 for customer benefit
- **Eligibility**: Well-run, cost-efficient reactors
- **Impact**: Supports largest regulated nuclear fleet (11 GW)
