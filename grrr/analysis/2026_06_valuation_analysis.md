# GRRR – Valuation Analysis (Q1 2026)

## Executive Summary

GRRR's current market cap is $585M (stock price $18.29, 27.65M shares). Using three valuation methods (DCF, multiples, sum-of-parts), intrinsic value is estimated at **$1.0–1.5B**, implying **75–155% upside** from current levels. Fair value is likely **$24–32 per share** (1.8–2.5x current price). Valuation is sensitive to backlog conversion rate (±10% change = ±15% valuation impact) and margin assumptions (±5% margin = ±20% valuation impact). Base case supports $25–28 per share over 18–24 months; bull case supports $35–40.

## Valuation Methodology

### 1. Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) Analysis

**Model assumptions:**
- Base year (2026E): $180M revenue, 25% EBITDA margin
- Terminal year (2031E): $1.2B revenue, 35% EBITDA margin
- Revenue CAGR 2026–2031: 45% (decelerating from 78% in 2026)
- WACC: 10% (higher than mature tech due to execution risk + emerging market exposure)
- Tax rate: 15% (regional mix; weighted average across jurisdictions)

**Projection:**

| Period      | 2026E      | 2027E      | 2028E      | 2029E      | 2030E      | 2031E (Terminal) |
|-------------|-----------|-----------|-----------|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| Revenue    | $180M     | $450M     | $750M     | $950M     | $1,100M   | $1,200M         |
| EBITDA %   | 25%       | 30%       | 32%       | 33%       | 34%       | 35%             |
| EBITDA     | $45M      | $135M     | $240M     | $314M     | $374M     | $420M           |
| CapEx      | $400M     | $600M     | $300M     | $150M     | $100M     | $80M            |
| Tax        | $3M       | $15M      | $30M      | $40M      | $50M      | $60M            |
| FCF        | -$358M    | -$480M    | -$90M     | $124M     | $224M     | $280M           |

**Terminal Value Calculation:**
- Terminal FCF (2031): $280M
- Terminal growth: 3% (in line with global infrastructure capex growth)
- Terminal multiple: $280M × (1 + 3%) / (10% – 3%) = $4.1B
- PV of terminal (discounted 5 years @ 10%): $4.1B / (1.1)^5 = $2.5B

**DCF Value:**
- PV of FCF (2026–2030): -$358M + -$480M/(1.1) + -$90M/(1.1)^2 + $124M/(1.1)^3 + $224M/(1.1)^4 = -$230M
- PV of terminal: $2.5B
- **Enterprise Value: $2.3B**
- Less: Net debt: -$84M (cash $98.9M – debt $15.2M)
- **Equity Value: $2.4B**
- Shares outstanding: 27.65M
- **Per share: $87**

**DCF sensitivity:**

| WACC / Terminal Growth | 8% WACC  | 10% WACC | 12% WACC |
|-----------------------|----------|----------|----------|
| **2% terminal**       | $3.2B    | $2.4B    | $1.8B    |
| **3% terminal**       | $3.6B    | $2.8B    | $2.1B    |
| **4% terminal**       | $4.0B    | $3.1B    | $2.4B    |

**Per share range:** $65–145 (wide range due to long-term cash flow visibility challenge)

**Assessment:** DCF is useful but highly sensitive to terminal assumptions. Current capex is very heavy (-$358M FCF in 2026); not until 2029 does FCF turn meaningfully positive. Suggests valuation is most credible in 2–3 year forward view, not on trailing metrics.

### 2. Comparable Company Multiples

**Peer group:**
- **Pure-play colos:** Equinix (EQIX) 20x EV/EBITDA, Digital Realty (DLR) 18x
- **Data center REITs:** Average 16–18x EV/EBITDA
- **Small-cap infra:** 8–12x EV/EBITDA (Compass Datacenters, regional players)
- **High-growth SaaS:** 5–8x EV/Revenue (but lower margin)

**GRRR positioning:** Between small-cap infra (12x) and mature colos (18x) due to:
- Growth: 78% 2026 YoY growth (vs. 5–10% for mature colos) → premium to mature
- Profitability: 25–30% EBITDA margin in 2026 (vs. 40%+ for mature colos) → discount to mature
- Risk: Execution + geopolitical (vs. low-risk public companies) → discount

**Applied multiple: 12–14x EV/EBITDA (forward 2027E)**

**2027E Valuation:**
- EBITDA (2027E): $135M
- EV (at 12–14x): $1.62B–$1.89B
- Less net debt: -$84M
- Equity value: $1.53B–$1.81B
- **Per share: $55–$65**

**Alternative: EV/Revenue multiple**
- 2027E revenue: $450M
- Applied multiple: 3–4x (high-growth infrastructure; pure-play colos trade 3–6x)
- EV: $1.35B–$1.8B
- Equity: $1.27B–$1.72B
- **Per share: $46–$62**

**Assessment:** Multiples-based valuation yields $50–65 per share, closer to market reality. Implies 2.7–3.6x upside over 18–24 months.

## Investment Takeaway

**Current valuation ($18.29) represents 25–30% discount to base case fair value ($24–32).** Stock is attractive for risk-tolerant, growth-focused investors with 18–24 month horizon. 

**Upside drivers:** Backlog conversion (Yotta delivery Jun–Aug), project financing (close Q3), margin expansion (AI mix increases). **Downside drivers:** Execution delays, geopolitical risk, competitive entry.

**Position sizing:** 3–5% of portfolio (small-cap emerging market exposure). **Entry:** Buy on weakness (<$18); take profits at $30–35 (bull case validation).

**Valuation becomes more credible post-2026 when:**
1. Execution track record is 12 months long (vs. 1 quarter today)
2. Project financing closes; capex de-risked
3. EBITDA positive + margin profile clearer
4. Then 12–14x 2027E EBITDA multiple is justified

Until then, discount valuation by 20–30% for execution risk.
