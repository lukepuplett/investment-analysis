# GRRR – Financial Analysis (Q1 2026)

## Executive Summary

GRRR is at an inflection point: legacy business stabilized, AI infrastructure platform scaling. Q1 2026 marked a critical turning point—operating cash flow swung positive (+$6.6M vs. -$10.7M YoY), cash surged to $98.9M (+373% YoY), and receivables discipline tightened dramatically. Revenue grew 55% YoY to $28.2M in Q1, but reported losses of $41.1M mask the underlying operating reality: $22.2M in FX losses and $20.9M in deferred stock-based compensation account for 97%+ of the loss. Excluding these one-time charges, underlying operating loss was only $1.2M.

**Investment Grade:** Execution on backlog conversion and margin recovery is the key watch. GRRR has fortress balance sheet backing ($98.9M cash, minimal debt), visibility to $5B+ in signed contracts, and a clear path to profitability if gross margins expand from 30% to 60%+ as AI infrastructure revenue mix grows. Path credible.

## Revenue Composition & Growth Drivers

### Current State (TTM: $111.3M)
- **Legacy Business** (~30–40%): Security intelligence, network intelligence, smart city tech. Mature, declining slightly in absolute dollars but providing stable cash base.
- **AI Infrastructure** (60–70% of forward guidance): GPU-as-a-service, data center capacity, managed services. Started from near-zero 12 months ago; now signed contracts for $5B+ cumulative delivery.

### Q1 2026 Revenue Drivers
- **Yotta India GPU deployments**: First delivery July 2026; revenue recognition Sep–Nov 2026. Three phases totaling ~$3.2B contract value.
- **Thailand Korat 200-MW campus**: Land secured, power planning underway. Construction starts Q3/Q4 2026; revenue 2027+.
- **NeutraDC co-location (Southeast Asia)**: Live capacity online Q3/Q4 2026; revenue from same period.
- **Egypt project (legacy)**: Final implementation stage; all advance payment guarantees released (down from $50M+ to $45k). Recurring revenue begins post-completion (mid-2027).

### Forward Guidance
- **2026E:** $160–$200M (vs. $101.4M in 2025). Midpoint $180M = +78% growth.
- **2027E:** $500M revenue target (CEO conviction). Driven by Yotta full-year contribution, Korat ramp-up, co-location scaling.

## Working Capital Discipline

GRRR's working capital story is underappreciated. Advance payment guarantees collapsed from $50M+ to $45k—a signal that all major project milestones are behind them. This frees cash for growth.

- **Receivables:** $54.1M as of Q1 2026 (down sharply from $76M+ in prior quarters). Collections tightened; major customers paying on time.
- **Payables:** $43.9M; growing in line with scale. No distress signals.
- **Days Sales Outstanding (DSO):** Improved; moving toward net-positive cash conversion as scale increases.

Management explicitly stated: "Cash conversion will become the key metric. Revenue gets attention, but cash earns respect."

| Revenue                     | $101.4M    | $180M (est.) | $500M     |
| Gross margin %              | 33.4%      | 50–55%     | 60–70%    |

| EBITDA margin %             | 19.5% (adj.) | 25–30%     | 35–40%+   |

**Key Assumptions:**
1. AI infrastructure revenue mix rises to 70%+ of total by year-end 2026.
2. Gross margins on AI infra: 75–80% (vs. 30% blended in Q1).
3. SG&A leverage: hiring ramp decelerates in H2 2026; operational staff absorbed into cost base.
4. No further major FX hits (Egypt, Thailand stabilized as currencies).

**Critical Risk:** If backlog conversion slips (GPU supply delays, financing gaps), margin expansion timeline extends. If Yotta ramps on schedule, profitability visible by Q4 2026 EBITDA.

## Investment Takeaway

GRRR's financial profile is shifting from turnaround (prove-the-model) to scale (execute-the-backlog). Q1 2026 operating cash flow inflection is the key signal that the business is working—customers are paying, projects are delivering, and execution risk is declining. Gross margin compression in Q1 is temporary; once Yotta and co-location revenue mixes in (Sep–Dec), margins should recover dramatically. 

Balance sheet fortress-like. Cash runway is 18+ months at current burn; with backlog conversion, cash generation likely by Q4 2026. Path to profitability is credible if execution holds.
