## BLUF Summaries by Analysis Document

### Financial Analysis
**Status:** Revenue composition clearly shifting from legacy (30%) to AI infra (70% forward). Q1 2026 gross margin compressed to 30% due to hardware mobilization; recovery to 50–60% expected Q4 2026 as AI mix increases. Operating cash flow inflection (+$6.6M in Q1 vs. -$10.7M YoY) is validation of execution—customers paying, projects delivering. Working capital discipline excellent: advance payment guarantees down from $50M+ to $45k (Egypt project delivered). Path to profitability credible if backlog converts and margins expand. **Key watch:** Gross margin % by segment; target 40%+ blended by Q4 2026.

### Market Analysis
**Status:** GRRR capturing rare confluence: first-mover in sovereign AI infrastructure in Asia, strong customer anchor (Yotta, Thai govt, Egypt recurring), vertical integration + full-stack offering. AI infrastructure TAM in Asia = $3–5B by 2027; GRRR targeting $500M (10–17% market share) = reasonable. Demand drivers credible: hyperscaler saturation, govt AI sovereignty mandates, cost arbitrage (land/power in Thailand, India). Pricing power high: managed services premium vs. pure colos. **Key watch:** New customer wins outside of Yotta; backlog diversification.

### Competitive Analysis
**Status:** GRRR's moat is full-stack integration (infra + intelligence + managed services), not just data centers. Competitive position: 3.5/5 (strong in region, but unassailable). Durability: 3–5 years (until competitors replicate). Pure-play colos (Equinix) compete on commodity; hyperscalers (AWS) on vendor lock-in; GRRR wins on sovereignty + full-stack. **Key watch:** Equinix M&A activity in SEA; AWS expansion; regional consolidation among colos.

### Technical Analysis
**Status:** Three major projects on track: Yotta (Jul delivery), Korat (construction Q3/Q4), co-location (online Q3/Q4). GPU supply chain tight but manageable via Supermicro partnership. Project financing $500–800M in advanced docs; closure expected Q3 2026. Execution risk is organizational (hiring ramp, contractor coordination); no catastrophic technical risks. **Key watch:** Yotta delivery date (weekly); project financing term sheet finalization; Korat EPC contract.

### Risk Assessment
**Status:** Eight major risks audited. Highest severity: execution at scale (7/10), geopolitical exposure (7/10), GPU supply (6/10), project financing (6/10). Mitigants credible for most risks; geopolitical exposure is uncontrollable. **Red flag threshold:** Yotta Phase 1 slips >8 weeks, project financing not closed by Q3, FX losses >$15M in single quarter, customer cancellation, revenue miss >15% vs. guidance.

### Investment Thesis
**Status:** Bull case credible; execution unproven at scale. Path to $500M revenue + 35% EBITDA by 2027 = $1.2–1.5B valuation (2–2.5x upside). Base case: $160–$200M 2026, $350M 2027 = 1.8x upside. Bear case: execution stumbles = 30–45% downside. Conviction: 7.5/10 (base case). Recommendation: BUY for growth + infrastructure investors; 3–5% allocation; 18–24 month hold. Exit triggers: Yotta slip >8 weeks, project financing delay, revenue miss >15%.

### Valuation Analysis
**Status:** Fair value $25–30 per share (base case), $35–40 (bull), $12–15 (bear). DCF yields $87 (very sensitive to terminal assumptions); multiples (12–14x 2027E EBITDA) yield $55–65; SOTP yields $39. Current price $18.29 represents 25–30% discount to fair value. Key sensitivity: ±$100M in 2027E revenue = ±$13 per share; ±5% margin = ±$9 per share. Multiple is most sensitive lever. **Action:** Buy at <$20; take profits at $30–35 (bull case validation).
