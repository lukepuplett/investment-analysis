## Executive Summary (BLUF)

**Amazon.com is the world's dominant e-commerce and cloud infrastructure platform with $670B TTM revenue, trading at 3.7x P/S ($2.44T market cap, $237 current price) on secular AI/cloud tailwinds BUT facing a capital-intensive investment cycle that has compressed FCF from $32.9B (2024) to $10.6B (TTM). AWS ($132B annualized run rate, 20.2% YoY growth) generates 60%+ of operating income at 34.6% margins and is re-accelerating on AI infrastructure demand (Trainium chips fully subscribed at 30-40% better price/performance, capacity monetizing immediately per management). However, $125B+ CapEx in 2025 (increasing 2026) creates near-term FCF pressure while North America retail operates at only 4.5% margins (6.9% ex-FTC settlement). Advertising ($17.6B Q3, +22% YoY, 3rd consecutive quarter acceleration) provides high-margin growth kicker with full-funnel differentiation. Key risks: AWS growth deceleration from Azure/GCP competition (severity 9/10, mitigant strength 7/10), sustained CapEx burden delaying FCF inflection beyond 2027 (severity 9/10, mitigant strength 6/10), retail margin compression preventing 6-8% target (severity 8/10, mitigant strength 6/10), regulatory breakup risk (severity 7/10). Valuation: Probability-weighted fair value $225/share (Bull 25% × $290, Base 50% × $220, Bear 25% × $160). Current price $237 offers limited margin of safety (5% overvalued vs fair value). Conviction: 7/10 (Base Case). Recommendation: Hold current positions; add on weakness to $210-220; strict stop-loss <$185 (AWS deceleration signal). Target: $290 Bull Case (2027E, 25% probability - AWS dominance + retail margin expansion), $220 Base Case (2026E, 50% probability - current trajectory), $160 Bear Case (2026E, 25% probability - AWS share loss + CapEx burden). Portfolio weighting: 8-10% core holding.**
