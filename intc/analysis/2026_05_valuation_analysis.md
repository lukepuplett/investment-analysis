## Valuation Conclusion

Intel's valuation of **8.3x P/S and 42.8x EV/EBITDA is aggressive** for a company that is:
- Currently unprofitable at operating level
- Generating negative free cash flow
- Facing significant execution risks
- Operating in markets with structural headwinds (CPU share loss to AMD)

**Fair value is likely $65-85** (base case) with downside to $30-50 (bear case) if execution fails.

**Current price of $94.75 implies:**
- Probability of bull case success: 60%+ (market valuation is very optimistic)
- Margin of safety: **None** (downside risk substantially exceeds upside)
- Suitable for: Only high-conviction turnaround investors with 3+ year time horizon

**For most investors, valuation is unattractive.** Better entry points would be:
- $75-80 (base case fair value): Good risk/reward
- $60-65 (execution clarifies): Excellent risk/reward
- <$50 (turnaround fails): Distressed opportunity
