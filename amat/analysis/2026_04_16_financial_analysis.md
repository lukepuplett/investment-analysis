# Applied Materials, Inc. (AMAT) – Financial Analysis

**Date:** April 16, 2026
**Data Source:** Q1 FY26 earnings call (Feb 12, 2026), Yahoo Finance (April 16, 2026)

---

## Executive Summary

**BLUF: Applied Materials delivered Q1 FY26 revenue of $7.0B (flat YoY) and EPS of $2.38, with gross margin expanding to 49.1% (+70 bps YoY). Guidance for Q2 FY26 projects revenue of $7.65B (±$500M, ~9% sequential growth) and EPS of $2.64 (±$0.20). The company is generating strong cash flows ($1.69B operating cash flow in Q1, $1.0B free cash flow) while investing heavily in capacity expansion and R&D. Valuation appears stretched at 40.4x trailing P/E but justified by 20%+ expected 2026 growth and market leadership in AI infrastructure equipment.**

- **Q2 FY26 Guidance (Base):** Management guidance of $7.65B ±$500M; represents ~9% sequential growth
- **FY2026 Growth Driver:** >20% semi equipment growth driven by AI infrastructure investment, weighted toward H2
- **Clean Room Capacity:** Primary limiting factor; availability expected to improve through 2026
- **China Risk:** Revenue mix at 27% of SSG+AGS sales; potential trade restriction headwinds in bear case

## Key Financial Metrics to Monitor

### Quarter-to-Quarter Indicators
- **Revenue Guidance vs. Delivery:** Track if subsequent quarters meet/exceed $7.65B Q2 base case
- **Gross Margin Trajectory:** 49.3% guidance for Q2; any decline below 49% would be concerning
- **Free Cash Flow Conversion:** Monitor if FCF conversion holds at 14-19% as inventory levels normalize
- **Bookings vs. Revenue:** Not disclosed quarterly; track through customer announcements for lead indicators

### Annual Performance Targets
- **FY2026 Revenue:** $30.2B (base case) implies 20%+ growth; requires strong H2 execution
- **Operating Margin:** 29%+ for full year; currently tracking well
- **Free Cash Flow:** $6-7B annually at 20%+ growth; supports continued 85%+ shareholder returns

### External Risk Monitors
- **China Exposure:** 27% of SSG+AGS sales; monitor for trade restrictions or customer cancellations
- **Clean Room Capacity:** Primary growth constraint; track fab utilization rates and new fab announcements
- **Competitive Share Losses:** Watch for customer wins/losses at major foundries and memory manufacturers
