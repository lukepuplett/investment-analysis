# Applied Materials, Inc. (AMAT)

## Company Overview

Applied Materials, Inc. (AMAT) is a leading global supplier of manufacturing equipment, services, and software used in the production of semiconductor devices. The company provides process equipment for semiconductor front-end manufacturing (deposition, etch, and metrology) and back-end packaging, along with advanced services and software solutions that enable customers to accelerate learning and improve productivity.

**Ticker:** AMAT

## Current Market Dynamics: The Cleanroom Constraint (Why Q1 Revenue is Declining)

### The Supply Chain Dependency Chain

```

```

### The Phase Cycle: Why Q1 Looks Bad But FY26 Will Be Strong

| Phase | Timeline | What's Happening | Revenue Impact | AMAT Status |
|-------|----------|------------------|----------------|------------|
| **Order Phase** | 2024-2025 | Fabs order equipment aggressively | Revenue ↑ | Orders booked |
| **Delivery Phase** | 2025-Q1 FY26 | Equipment ships; inventory builds at fabs | Revenue ↓ | Digesting prior orders |
| **Installation Phase** | Q2-Q3 FY26+ | Cleanrooms come online; equipment finally installed | Revenue ↑ | Cash collection accelerates |
| **Next Order Phase** | H2 2026+ | Fabs order MORE equipment for next generation/next fab | Revenue ↑↑ | New backlog |

**Current Status (Q1 FY26):** Stuck in **Delivery/Digestion Phase**
- Equipment already shipped (cash collected)
- Fabs have inventory sitting idle (no cleanroom space yet)
- No new orders yet (fabs can't install more equipment)
- **This is temporary** – cleanroom construction finishing Q2-Q3

**Why Management is Confident About H2 2026:**
> "Our customers are giving us increased longer-term visibility... Based on this visibility, we expect strong growth momentum into 2027."

**Translation:** "We know exactly when cleanrooms open. Orders will flow when space is available."

### Why This Explains Everything

**Question:** Why is SSG down 8% in an AI boom?
**Answer:** Not demand destruction—inventory digestion. Equipment already delivered, waiting for cleanroom space.

**Question:** Why does Q1 look weak but guidance is strong?
**Answer:** Q1 is the trough of the digestion phase. H2 is when cleanrooms open and new orders pour in.

**Question:** Why would AMAT have pricing power despite weak Q1?
**Answer:** Pricing power is based on scarcity (equipment is scarce), not current order flow. Once cleanrooms open, fabs will pay premium prices to jump the queue.

## Business Segments

### Semiconductor Systems (SSG)
Primary business providing deposition, etch, metrology, and inspection equipment for semiconductor manufacturing across:
- **Leading-Edge Logic** (#1 position with strong leadership in deposition, etch, and e-beam)
- **Memory (DRAM/HBM)** (#1 position with leadership in materials deposition for wiring and patterning)
- **Advanced Packaging** (#1 position in HBM and 3D chiplet stacking)
- **NAND** (modest position, expected flat/modest growth in 2026)
- **ICAPS** (IoT, communications, automotive, power, sensor markets - expected flat in 2026)

Service and software business supporting installed base of 55,000+ tools globally:
- Equipment maintenance and support
- Advanced process diagnostics and control (PDC/e-beam metrology)
- AI-powered software solutions (AIx - Actionable Insight Accelerator)
- Installation and field services

Q1 FY26 Revenue: $1.56B (+15% YoY)
Gross Margin: ~40%
Key Metrics: 2.9-year average contract, 90% renewal rate, 2/3 under contract

## Strategic Initiatives

### 1. Inflection-Focused Innovation
- R&D focused on high-value solutions enabling major device architecture inflections
- >12 new products launching in 2026 including:
  - **Viva radical treatment system** - angstrom-level precision for gate-all-around transistors
  - **Sym3 Z Magnum** - advanced conductor etch for GAA and DRAM
  - **Spectral ALD system** - selective deposition of monocrystalline moly (15% contact resistance reduction)

### 2. EPIC Platform (Emerging later in 2026)
- Co-innovation center in Silicon Valley
- Brings Applied R&D innovators together with key customer and partner innovators
- Provides customers earlier access to R&D portfolio
- Enables faster learning cycles and multi-node visibility for Applied

### 3. Advanced Services Growth
- AIx software connecting 30,000+ chambers with AI-powered monitoring
- 30% faster response times enabling increased wafer output
- Automated distribution centers with AI-enabled robotics
- Service business targeting double-digit growth rates

## Market Outlook

### 2026 Expectations
- **Semi Equipment Growth:** >20% for AMAT
- **WFE Intensity:** ~15% of semiconductor industry revenue
- **Industry Revenue:** Potentially reaching $1 trillion (earlier than previously expected)
- **Growth Drivers:** AI infrastructure investment accelerating
- **Demand Profile:** Weighted to second half; clean room space availability pacing growth

### Key Market Segments
1. **Leading-Edge Logic** (fastest growing)
   - FinFET capacity additions + gate-all-around ramps
   - Applied market share target: >50% in GAA and wiring/backside power

2. **HBM DRAM** (highest growth in memory)
   - 3x-4x more wafer starts per delivered bit vs. standard DRAM
   - Die stacking increasing from 12 to 16 to 20+ dies
   - Applied #1 in DRAM equipment

3. **Advanced Packaging** (rapidly growing)
   - HBM and 3D chiplet stacking as fastest-growing segments
   - Applied #1 in advanced packaging

4. **NAND** (modest growth)
   - Expected <10% of total WFE in 2026
   - Primarily upgrades and conversions

5. **ICAPS** (flat in 2026)
   - Following elevated spending in prior years
   - Both globally and in China expected flat

### 2027 Outlook
- Strong momentum expected to continue into 2027
- Clean room capacity constraints expected to persist
- Multiple factory projects coming online
- Continued strong AI infrastructure investment
