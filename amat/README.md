# Applied Materials, Inc. (AMAT)

## Company Overview

Applied Materials, Inc. (AMAT) is a leading global supplier of manufacturing equipment, services, and software used in the production of semiconductor devices. The company provides process equipment for semiconductor front-end manufacturing (deposition, etch, and metrology) and back-end packaging, along with advanced services and software solutions that enable customers to accelerate learning and improve productivity.

## Current Market Dynamics: The Cleanroom Constraint (Why Q1 Revenue is Declining)

### The Supply Chain Dependency Chain

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐

                 │                       │

```

### The Phase Cycle: Why Q1 Looks Bad But FY26 Will Be Strong

| Phase | Timeline | What's Happening | Revenue Impact | AMAT Status |
|-------|----------|------------------|----------------|------------|
| **Order Phase** | 2024-2025 | Fabs order equipment aggressively | Revenue ↑ | Orders booked |
| **Delivery Phase** | 2025-Q1 FY26 | Equipment ships; inventory builds at fabs | Revenue ↓ | Digesting prior orders |
| **Installation Phase** | Q2-Q3 FY26+ | Cleanrooms come online; equipment finally installed | Revenue ↑ | Cash collection accelerates |
| **Next Order Phase** | H2 2026+ | Fabs order MORE equipment for next generation/next fab | Revenue ↑↑ | New backlog |

**Current Status (Q1 FY26):** Stuck in **Delivery/Digestion Phase**
- Equipment already shipped (cash collected)
- Fabs have inventory sitting idle (no cleanroom space yet)
- No new orders yet (fabs can't install more equipment)
- **This is temporary** – cleanroom construction finishing Q2-Q3

**Why Management is Confident About H2 2026:**
> "Our customers are giving us increased longer-term visibility... Based on this visibility, we expect strong growth momentum into 2027."

**Translation:** "We know exactly when cleanrooms open. Orders will flow when space is available."

### Why This Explains Everything

**Question:** Why is SSG down 8% in an AI boom?
**Answer:** Not demand destruction—inventory digestion. Equipment already delivered, waiting for cleanroom space.

**Question:** Why does Q1 look weak but guidance is strong?
**Answer:** Q1 is the trough of the digestion phase. H2 is when cleanrooms open and new orders pour in.

**Question:** Why would AMAT have pricing power despite weak Q1?
**Answer:** Pricing power is based on scarcity (equipment is scarce), not current order flow. Once cleanrooms open, fabs will pay premium prices to jump the queue.

- **Foundry, Logic & Other** (67% of SSG revenue) – #1 position in deposition, etch, and e-beam metrology

## Strategic Initiatives

### 1. EPIC Center Ecosystem (Launched Q2 FY26)
**Co-innovation platform designed to accelerate commercialization of next-generation semiconductor technologies:**
- **TSMC Partnership** – Joint innovation in materials engineering, equipment innovation, and process integration for AI-era chips
- **SK hynix Collaboration** – Side-by-side engineering at EPIC Center on next-generation DRAM and HBM for AI and high-performance computing
- **Micron Technology** – Co-development of next-generation DRAM, HBM, and NAND solutions for energy-efficient AI systems
- **University Partnerships** – ASU, RPI, Stanford as inaugural research partners
- **Advantest Integration** – Linking front-end manufacturing technologies with back-end testing to accelerate design-to-market timelines
- **Impact:** Reduces time from early-stage research to full-scale manufacturing commercialization

### 2. Advanced Packaging Expansion
- **NEXX Acquisition** – Agreement to acquire ASMPT's NEXX business (large-area advanced packaging deposition equipment)
- Broadens panel-level advanced packaging portfolio
- Enables chipmakers/systems companies to build larger-body AI accelerators
- Positions AMAT for growing HBM stacking and 3D chiplet markets

### 3. Inflection-Focused Innovation
- >12 new product launches in 2026 including:
  - **Precision Selective Nitride PECVD** – Atomic-level precision for 3D Gate-All-Around transistors
  - **Trillium ALD** – Silicon nanosheet wrapping with complex metal gate stacks for AI computing
  - Advanced conductor etch for GAA and DRAM patterning

### 4. Advanced Services & Software Growth
- **AIx Software** connecting 30,000+ chambers with AI-powered monitoring
- 30% faster response times enabling increased wafer output
- Automated distribution centers with AI-enabled robotics
- Service business targeting double-digit growth rates
- 90%+ customer renewal rates on multi-year contracts

## Market Outlook

### Calendar 2026 Expectations (from May 14, 2026 guidance)
- **AMAT Semi Equipment Growth:** >30% (raised from >20% in April)
- **CEO Quote:** "The rapid global build-out of AI computing infrastructure combined with Applied's strong leadership positions in leading-edge logic, DRAM and advanced packaging provide an exceptionally strong foundation for sustained, multi-year revenue and profit growth."
- **CFO Quote:** "As the largest process equipment company in the fastest growing markets, our top priority is ensuring we have the operational and supply chain readiness to support our customers' growth. We have increased our build plan, inventory positions and logistics capacity."
- **Growth Driver:** AI infrastructure investment now "in full force" rather than anticipated
- **Demand Profile:** Strong momentum across H2 2026 and into 2027

### Key Market Segments (Q2 FY26 Mix)
1. **Foundry & Logic** (67% of SSG revenue) – Fastest growing
   - Gate-all-around (GAA) transistor inflection at advanced nodes
   - Applied #1 in deposition, etch, e-beam metrology
   - Market share target: >50% in GAA and wiring/backside power

2. **HBM DRAM** (29% of SSG revenue) – Highest growth in memory
   - 3x-4x more wafer starts per delivered bit vs. standard DRAM
   - Die stacking increasing from 12 to 16 to 20+ dies
   - Applied #1 in DRAM materials deposition and advanced packaging
   - Critical bottleneck for AI accelerator performance

3. **Advanced Packaging** (embedded in Logic/DRAM) – Rapidly growing
   - HBM and 3D chiplet stacking as fastest-growing segments
   - Applied #1 with NEXX acquisition expanding portfolio
   - Enables larger-body AI accelerators

4. **NAND** (4% of SSG revenue) – Modest growth
   - Expected modest growth in 2026
   - Primarily upgrades and conversions

### 2027+ Outlook
- Strong momentum expected to continue through 2027 and beyond
- Supply chain capacity and cleanroom space remain primary constraints
- Multiple factory projects coming online globally
- Sustained AI infrastructure investment and data center expansion
