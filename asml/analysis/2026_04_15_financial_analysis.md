## Revenue Analysis

### Full Year 2026 Guidance (Raised)
**EUR 36-40 Billion** (previously lower guidance)

ASML significantly raised full-year revenue expectations based on:
- **AI-driven demand acceleration** for advanced memory and logic capacity
- **Customer capacity expansion** across both DRAM and advanced logic (2nm and beyond)
- **Increased lithography adoption** (EUV and immersion technology penetration)
- **Installed base strength** providing near-term capacity solution

| Component | Status | Driver |

**Key Insight:** Installed base revenue (EUR 2.5B in Q1) is becoming increasingly important as customers seek rapid capacity deployment. This high-margin business is less capital-intensive for customers than new system purchases.

## Risks to Financial Performance

### Downside Scenarios
1. **Demand Cliff:** If AI capex cycle moderates faster than expected
2. **Margin Compression:** From mix shift or competitive pricing pressure
3. **Working Capital Swings:** Large customer advance payments can fluctuate
4. **Export Controls:** Geopolitical restrictions on Chinese sales
5. **Capex Delays:** Customer postponement of capacity investments

### Mitigants
- Diversified customer base across memory and logic
- Strong contract backlog and long-term commitments
- Technology differentiation (EUV, High NA) limits competition
- Installed base provides predictable recurring revenue
- Conservative balance sheet provides cushion

## Conclusion

ASML demonstrates exceptional financial health with strong revenue growth momentum (28.5% YoY), industry-leading profitability (31.2% operating margin), and robust cash generation ($7.0B FCF TTM). The company's conservative balance sheet (0.11 D/E, 3.19 current ratio, $4.7B net cash) and operational leverage position it well to capitalize on the AI-driven semiconductor capacity cycle. FY 2026 revenue guidance of EUR 36-40B represents significant growth from prior expectations, supported by customer demand, technology adoption acceleration, and installed base strength. Primary financial risks relate to demand cycle sustainability and geopolitical factors, but current indicators suggest strong earnings trajectory through at least 2026-2027.
