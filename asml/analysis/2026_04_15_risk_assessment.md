# ASML Risk Assessment – Q1 2026

## Executive Summary

**BLUF: ASML faces manageable risk profile with no critical unmitigated risks preventing investment. Primary risks—geopolitical export controls (severity 6/10, well-mitigated by guidance), semiconductor capex cycle moderation (severity 7/10, not imminent based on customer commentary), and High NA ecosystem delays (severity 5/10, showing positive progress)—are offset by strong competitive moats and customer visibility through 2027+. Conservative balance sheet (D/E 0.11, Net Cash $4.7B) provides downside protection. Key monitoring metrics include customer capex guidance, High NA customer bookings, and geopolitical developments. Overall risk posture: Moderate but well-managed.**

## Detailed Risk Analysis

### 1. AI Capex Cycle Moderation Risk

**Severity: 7/10** | **Mitigant Strength: 7/10** | **Status: ⚠️ Monitor**

#### Nature of Risk
Semiconductor equipment demand historically cycles every 3-5 years. If AI infrastructure investment moderates faster than expected (e.g., 2027-2028 rather than 2028-2030), ASML's revenue growth could sharply decelerate.

#### Current Evidence
**Positive Indicators:**
- Memory customers report being "sold out for 2026 and beyond"
- Advanced logic customers "building capacity for several nodes"
- Customer capital budgets committed for 2027+
- Long-term customer commitments backing capex

**Vulnerability:**
- If AI adoption proves slower than expected
- If hyperscaler capex cycles earlier than anticipated
- If market consolidation reduces fabric builder count

#### Mitigants
1. **Structural AI Demand:** AI isn't discretionary; it's becoming utility infrastructure
2. **Long-term Commitments:** Customer orders backed by multi-year agreements
3. **Geopolitical Diversification:** US fab expansion (CHIPS Act) + Taiwan alternatives
4. **Diverse End Markets:** Not just hyperscaler AI, but enterprise, automotive, IoT
5. **High NA Extension:** Even if cycle moderates, High NA provides new driver

#### Monitoring Metrics
| Metric | Target | Current | Monitoring Frequency |
|--------|--------|---------|----------------------|
| Customer capex guidance | Growing 2027 | Expected strong | Quarterly earnings |
| Semiconductor fab utilization | >85% | ~90% | Quarterly industry reports |
| Memory chip spot prices | Stable | Elevated | Monthly |
| AI chip demand trends | Sustained growth | Strong | Monthly industry tracking |
| ASML guidance revisions | Maintained or raised | Raised in Q1 | Quarterly |

#### Scenario Analysis
- **Bull Case:** AI capex extends through 2028, cycle peaks 2027-2028, ASML revenue $40B+ in 2026 sustained into 2028-2029
- **Base Case:** Cycle moderates 2028, peak to trough over 12 months, revenue declines 10-20% but remains elevated
- **Bear Case:** Cycle compresses 2027, steep decline 2027-2028, revenue drops 30-40%, but ASML competitive position unchanged

### 2. China Export Controls Risk

**Severity: 6/10** | **Mitigant Strength: 8/10** | **Status: ✓ Mitigated**

#### Nature of Risk
US export controls restrict ASML's ability to sell advanced lithography equipment to Chinese semiconductor manufacturers. This reduces addressable market and could impact revenue growth assumptions.

#### Current Status (Q1 2026)
- Export control discussions ongoing
- ASML's FY 2026 guidance (EUR 36-40B) "accommodates potential outcomes of the export control discussions"
- Suggests management already discounted Chinese sales impact

#### Market Impact Assessment
| Customer | ASML Exposure | Export Control Impact | Alternative |
|----------|---------------|----------------------|-------------|
| SMIC (China Advanced) | $500M-$1B/year est. | Likely restricted | SMIC can source older DUV systems |
| YMTC (China Memory) | $300-500M/year est. | Likely restricted | Limited options, capex constrained |
| CXMT (China Memory) | $100-300M/year est. | Likely restricted | Limited options |
| Chinese fabless | Indirect (through TSMC/Samsung) | No direct impact | No change |

**TAM Impact:** ~$1-2B annual revenue at risk if full restrictions implemented

#### Mitigants
1. **Already in Guidance:** Management explicitly stated guidance accommodates export controls
2. **Customer Diversification:** 70%+ revenue from US/EU/Korea/Japan (less likely to be restricted)
3. **Mature Tech Fallback:** China can use older DUV technology for non-cutting-edge nodes
4. **Supply Constraint Offset:** With constrained supply, losing China sales frees capacity for Western customers
5. **Geopolitical Tailwind:** US fab expansion (CHIPS Act) offsets some China loss

#### Historical Precedent
- ASML managed semiconductor equipment export restrictions to Japan (1980s-1990s)
- US chip manufacturers adjusted to restricted markets
- Business model resilient to geographic restrictions

#### Monitoring Metrics
| Metric | Current | Monitoring |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| Regulatory guidance | Expected by Q2/Q3 2026 | Policy developments |
| ASML China revenue % | ~5-10% estimate | Quarterly disclosure |
| Western customer demand | Robust, growing | Quarterly earnings |
| Guidance revisions | None yet | Quarterly |

**Assessment:** Well-mitigated and likely already priced into current guidance.

### 3. High NA Ecosystem Maturity Risk

**Severity: 5/10** | **Mitigant Strength: 8/10** | **Status: ✓ On Track**

#### Nature of Risk
High NA EUV requires full ecosystem maturity: resist formulations, masks, metrology, process development. If ecosystem isn't ready for 2027 volume ramp, customer adoption could delay 1-2 years.

#### Current Status (SPIE 2026 Evidence)
**Positive Evidence:**
- Resist partners demonstrated 18nm line (logic), 28nm hole (memory) readiness
- Mask makers progressing on High NA mask technology
- Early customer systems in operation with positive feedback
- 3-4 node extension confirmed viable with ecosystem partners

**Timeline:**
- Q1 2026: Early customer systems operational
- 2026-2027: Ramp phase for ecosystem
- 2027+: Volume production expected

#### Mitigants
1. **Early Customer Collaboration:** ASML engaged with customers on ecosystem
2. **SPIE 2026 Validation:** Customer public presentations confirming progress
3. **Resist Partner Investment:** Dupont, Shin-Etsu investing in High NA resist
4. **Mask Maker Readiness:** ASML supplying mask development tools
5. **Fallback Option:** Low NA continues improving (1,000W source), High NA optional

#### Risk Mitigation Impact
- **If High NA delays 6-12 months:** Customer adoption timeline extends slightly, not material to ASML
- **If High NA delays 18-24 months:** Competitive advantage extends further (Nikon/Canon not ready either)
- **Precedent:** EUV Low NA delayed multiple times (2000-2013), didn't prevent success

#### Monitoring Metrics
| Metric | Current Status | Monitoring |
|--------|----------------|-----------|
| High NA customer orders | Growing 2027 ramp | Quarterly earnings |
| Resist readiness | SPIE confirmed | Customer feedback |
| Mask maker timeline | On track | ASML supplier discussions |
| Metrology solutions | Developing | Customer partnerships |
| Customer samples in field | Multiple systems | Site visits/industry news |

**Assessment:** On track with good visibility, low probability of material delay.

### 4. Immersion DUV Recovery Uncertainty

**Severity: 4/10** | **Mitigant Strength: 7/10** | **Status: ✓ Recovering**

#### Nature of Risk
Immersion DUV demand was weak in 2024-2025. If recovery doesn't materialize, non-EUV revenue could underperform, impacting total company guidance.

#### Current Evidence (Q1 2026 Update)
**Positive Indicators:**
- Management: "Immersion demand has now reversed itself"
- Guidance: "Still expecting to get pretty close to immersion sales from last year in unit numbers" (despite slow start)
- 2027 outlook: "Increasing our move rate quarter-on-quarter"
- Management confidence: "Strong" based on earnings tone

#### Addressable Market
- Immersion DUV TAM: ~$4-5B globally
- ASML share: ~40% (~$1.5-2B)
- Growth driver: Non-EUV advanced logic, mature node volume

#### Mitigants
1. **Non-EUV Guidance:** Management now expecting "increase in non-EUV business" (vs. prior flat expectation)
2. **Customer Diversification:** Immersion used across multiple fab types
3. **Pricing:** DUV margins still strong (~50%), not margin-dependent on volume
4. **EUV Shift:** If immersion recovers slowly, EUV gains offset (higher margins)
5. **Market Drivers:** Automotive, IoT chips still driving non-EUV demand

#### 2026 Capacity Plans
- "Despite slow start, still expecting pretty close to immersion sales that we had last year in terms of unit numbers"
- **Interpretation:** Management confident in recovery, capacity allocated accordingly

### 5. Supply Chain Concentration Risk

**Severity: 5/10** | **Mitigant Strength: 8/10** | **Status: ✓ Secure**

#### Nature of Risk
ASML depends on Zeiss (EUV optics) as single supplier. If Zeiss capacity constrained or partnership disrupted, ASML production could be limited.

#### Critical Dependency: Zeiss EUV Optics
| Component | Dependency | Alternative | Risk Level |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| EUV Mirrors | 100% Zeiss | None (patents/IP) | Critical |
| Lithography optics | 100% Zeiss | None | Critical |
| DUV optics | ASML internal | Zeiss backup | Moderate |

#### Zeiss Partnership Strength
1. **Joint Development:** ASML and Zeiss jointly developing High NA optics
2. **Strategic Alignment:** Zeiss invested $1B+ in EUV optics, committed to success
3. **No Alternatives:** Zeiss is the only viable supplier for EUV reflective optics
4. **Market Incentives:** EUV success benefits both companies (Zeiss revenue + royalties)
5. **Geographical Alignment:** Both Germany/Netherlands (aligned interests)

#### Mitigants
1. **Exclusive Relationship:** Zeiss unlikely to support ASML competitors
2. **Long-term Agreements:** Multi-year supply contracts securing capacity
3. **Capacity Investment:** Zeiss investing in manufacturing capacity for High NA
4. **Partnership Lock-in:** 20+ years of joint development, deep integration
5. **Strategic Importance:** EUV success in mutual interest of both companies

#### Contingency Planning (ASML Perspective)
- ASML likely has strategic inventory of critical optics
- Multiple Zeiss facilities providing geographic redundancy
- Ongoing R&D likely exploring optics alternatives (long-term risk mitigation)

#### Monitoring Metrics
| Metric | Current | Monitoring |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| Zeiss capacity growth | Expanding | Quarterly partnerships |
| Lead times | Normal | Supply chain tracking |
| Partnership announcements | Positive (High NA) | Quarterly announcements |
| Geopolitical risks to Zeiss | Low (Germany/EU) | Geopolitical news |

**Assessment:** Well-mitigated through exclusive partnership and mutual strategic interest.

Overall risk posture is Moderate but well-managed with clear mitigants in place.
