# Rocket Lab (RKLB) Risk Assessment – Q4 2025
**Analysis Date:** February 26, 2026

---

## Executive Summary (BLUF)

**Rocket Lab faces EXISTENTIAL binary risk on Neutron (works operationally by Q4 2026 or company becomes $4-8B defense supplier, not $20B+ launch leader): Composite structures at scale, novel reusability architecture, and Hungry Hippo fairing create technical risks unmatched by Electron's maturity. Neutron delay beyond Q2 2027 or first-flight failure requires immediate position reduction and thesis reassessment. Electron's economic viability questionable long-term (SpaceX Falcon 9 rideshare pricing $275-300k/kg caps TAM expansion), though strategically important. Government funding uncertainty (SDA budget cycles) poses medium-term revenue risk. Space Systems backlog ($1B+) provides near-term cash generation and reduces near-term bankruptcy risk, but concentration in government contracts creates policy risk. Recommendation: Neutron success is make-or-break; establish strict monitoring and cut-loss triggers on technical failures or schedule slips beyond Q1 2027.**

## Conclusion

Rocket Lab faces EXISTENTIAL binary risk on Neutron (success by Q4 2026 or company becomes $4-8B defense supplier, not $20B+ launch leader). Electron is strategically important but economically challenged by SpaceX Falcon 9 rideshare pricing. Government contracts provide near-term revenue stability and reduce bankruptcy risk, but policy/budget uncertainty poses medium-term headwind.

Space Systems backlog ($1B+) and acquisition strategy (GEOST, OSI, PCL) are correct moves toward higher-margin, integrated product mix. However, integration execution risk is real; margin targets must be validated quarterly.

**Key Risk Summary:**
- **Existential:** Neutron success is make-or-break (Q4 2026 decision point)
- **Major:** Government funding cycles, acquisition integration
- **Medium:** Competitive intensity, Electron economics, supply chain
- **Outcome:** RKLB is binary bet on Neutron; appropriate only for investors tolerating 40-50% downside risk for 50-75% upside potential
