# Rocket Lab (RKLB) Investment Thesis – Q4 2025
**Analysis Date:** February 26, 2026

---

## BLUF (Bottom Line Up Front)

**Rocket Lab is a capital-intensive aerospace company at an inflection point: Space Systems (>50% of revenue) provides near-term cash generation and is maturing into profitability (40%+ margins); Electron demonstrates operational excellence (100% success, 21 missions in 2025) but faces structural economic pressure from SpaceX Falcon 9 rideshare pricing—strategically important but not financially accretive long-term. Neutron development is the existential bet: success by Q4 2026 unlocks 5–10x revenue upside ($1B+ medium-lift TAM) and validates $20B+ market cap; failure delays program 12–24 months and cedes market to Relativity/New Glenn, reducing RKLB to $4–8B defense supplier. At $73.60/share ($41.3B market cap, 40x P/S TTM revenue), current price offers NO margin of safety. Management's deliberate Q4 2026 first-flight target reflects engineering discipline (positive signal), but Neutron remains binary make-or-break event. Conviction: 5/10 (Base Case). Recommendation: HOLD for Neutron milestones; WAIT for $50–55 entry zone; REDUCE or EXIT if first flight slips to Q1 2027+.**

## Investment Thesis Summary

### Thesis Pillar 1: Space Systems Growth Engine (✅ Confirmed)

**Status:** ✅ On Track
**Confidence:** 8/10
**Evidence:**
- Q4 2025 Space Systems revenue >$75M (estimated ~50% of total $180M)
- SDA Tranche 3 award ($816M prime + ~$200M subsystem upside) provides 2+ years of growth visibility
- GEOST integration delivering higher margins (40%+ vs. 25-30% on Electron)
- Management guidance: Space Systems to be largest revenue driver by 2027

**Risk if Wrong:** Space Systems margin targets missed; acquisitions fail to integrate; SDA contract execution delays
**Monitoring KPI:** Space Systems gross margin (must stay ≥38%); quarterly revenue run rate ($100M+ by Q4 2026)

---

### Thesis Pillar 2: Electron Strategic Moat (✅ Confirmed)

**Status:** ✅ Intact, but Economic Viability Questionable
**Confidence:** 7/10
**Evidence:**
- 100% launch success rate; 21 missions in 2025; 7 in Q4 (record cadence)
- Only dedicated small-lift provider with operational cadence (competitors still in development)
- Returning customer growth (BlackSky 17 missions, others bulk buying)
- Government classifications and time-critical missions justify premium pricing

**Risk if Wrong:** SpaceX Falcon 9 rideshare economics ($275-300k/kg) commoditize small-lift; RKLB margin compression below 20%
**Monitoring KPI:** Electron ASP trend (must stay ≥$10M/mission); backlog conversion rate

---

### Thesis Pillar 3: Neutron as 5–10x Revenue Multiplier (⚠️ At Risk)

**Status:** ⚠️ CRITICAL EXECUTION RISK
**Confidence:** 4/10 (Binary outcome)
**Evidence:**
- $2-3B addressable medium-lift TAM (government + commercial)
- First-mover advantage: 12-18 months ahead of Relativity Space, New Glenn
- Government demand: SDA, Golden Dome, NASA programs support launch cadence
- Management target: 5-10 launches/year by 2028-2029 = $300-800M incremental revenue

**Risk if Wrong:** First flight failure; composite structure issues; cost overruns; schedule slips
- **Severity if Fails:** Thesis broken; RKLB valued as $4-8B defense supplier, not $20B+ launch leader
- **Financial Impact:** -$40-50/share
- **Probability of Success:** 60-65% (on track for Q4 2026 target)

**Monitoring KPI:** Static fire test results (Q1-Q2 2026), booster recovery validation, first flight readiness

### Thesis Pillar 4: Government Contracting Advantage (✅ Confirmed)
