## Valuation Methodology & Framework

### Why DCF Is Misleading for Binary Outcomes

RKLB's valuation is driven by a binary event (Neutron success or failure), making traditional DCF analysis misleading:

**DCF Assumptions Required:**
- Perpetual growth rate (assumes Neutron success continues indefinitely)
- Terminal EBITDA margin (assumes maturity 2030+, dependent on Neutron success)
- WACC (risk of failure not fully captured by discount rate alone)

**Why It Fails:**
- 40-50% probability of Neutron failure (not captured in WACC)
- Failure scenario reduces enterprise value 70-90% (DCF terminal value becomes irrelevant)
- Continuous compounding assumes no discontinuity; reality is binary jump event

**Conclusion:** Scenario analysis is primary valuation method; DCF is secondary check only

## Appendix: Valuation Models Used

### 1. Sum-of-Parts Valuation
- **Method:** Segment-level valuation; each business unit valued separately; sum for total EV
- **Strengths:** Captures optionality in Neutron; reflects different risk profiles by segment
- **Weaknesses:** Requires accurate segment growth assumptions; sensitive to multiple selection
- **Result:** Base Case $15-19B EV ($45-55/share)

### 2. Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)
- **Method:** Project revenue, EBITDA, capital expenditures, and terminal value; discount at WACC
- **Strengths:** Theoretically sound; captures long-term growth
- **Weaknesses:** Misleading for binary outcomes; assumes Neutron success; sensitive to terminal assumptions
- **Result:** $1.2-1.3B EV (undervalues growth; used as floor check)

### 3. Comparable Company Trading Multiples
- **Method:** Apply peer P/S and EV/EBITDA multiples to RKLB's revenue and EBITDA
- **Strengths:** Market-based; captures investor sentiment
- **Weaknesses:** RKLB has no true comparables; growth peers trade at 8-10x; defense contractors at 3-4x
- **Result:** 20-35x P/S range for bull case; 6-8x for bear case

### 4. Asset-Based Valuation
- **Method:** Value tangible assets (facilities, equipment, IP) and ongoing business value
- **Strengths:** Provides downside protection floor value
- **Weaknesses:** Assumes asset values don't decline; doesn't capture growth
- **Result:** $5.5-8.4B EV bear case ($12-20/share)
