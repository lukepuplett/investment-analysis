## Executive Summary

**BLUF: Emerson Electric operates in **attractive industrial automation markets** with **sustained demand** from energy security, power generation, and digital transformation trends. The company holds **strong market positions** in process automation (high market share), power generation (40%+ orders growth), and industrial software (growing ACV). **Market tailwinds** from LNG investments, data center power needs, and automation adoption support **mid-single digit growth** outlook. **Key risks** include China exposure, European weakness, and discrete market cyclicality. **BUY** - Beneficiary of structural industrial automation trends.**

Emerson Electric serves diverse industrial automation markets through two primary segments: Intelligent Devices (process automation, measurement, discrete automation) and Software and Control (control systems, software, test & measurement). The company benefits from structural trends including energy security, power generation expansion, digital transformation, and discrete market recovery.

## End-Market Analysis

### Process & Hybrid Markets

**Market Characteristics:**
- **Stability**: Less cyclical than discrete markets
- **MRO Focus**: 62% of sales from maintenance, repair, operations
- **Long Cycles**: Large capital projects with multi-year timelines
- **High Barriers**: Technology leadership and customer relationships critical

**Key End Markets:**

1. **Power Generation** (Strong Growth)
   - Underlying orders up 40% in Q3 2025
   - Expected to end year up 20%+
   - Drivers: Data center power needs, grid modernization, renewable integration
   - Geographic: Strong in Americas, Asia
   - **Outlook**: High teens growth sustainable for 24+ months

2. **LNG** (Robust Investment)
   - Global LNG capacity expansion
   - Strong demand from energy security initiatives
   - Multi-year project cycles
   - **Outlook**: Sustained mid-single digit growth

3. **Life Sciences** (Strong Growth)
   - Customer investments in biomedicines and GLP-one drugs
   - Biopharmaceutical capacity expansion
   - Regulatory-driven modernization
   - **Outlook**: Mid-single digit growth supported by demand

4. **Chemicals** (Mixed)
   - **Specialty Chemicals**: Performing well
   - **Bulk Chemicals**: Weak in Europe and China due to overcapacity
   - **Outlook**: Flat to negative for bulk, positive for specialty

5. **Oil & Gas** (Moderate Growth)
   - Upstream investments stable
   - Midstream/downstream modernization
   - **Outlook**: Low to mid-single digit growth

### Discrete Markets

**Market Characteristics:**
- **Cyclicality**: More sensitive to economic cycles
- **Recovery Phase**: Emerging from 2023-2024 downturn
- **Technology-Driven**: Innovation and product development critical

**Key End Markets:**

1. **Test & Measurement** (Strong Recovery)
   - Orders up 16% in Q3 2025
   - Expected to approach 20% growth in Q4
   - **Sub-markets**:
     - Aerospace & Defense: Strong, sustained momentum
     - Semiconductor: Validation and R&D investment recovery
     - Electronics: Broad-based recovery
     - Automotive: Improving from weak base
   - **Geographic**: Asia strongest (went down first, recovering fastest), North America solid, Europe lagging
   - **Outlook**: Double-digit growth expected near-term

2. **Discrete Automation** (Moderate Recovery)
   - Orders up 6% in Q3
   - **Challenges**: Automotive and packaging weak in Europe/China
   - **Strengths**: Broad-based industrials recovering
   - **Outlook**: Low to mid-single digit growth

3. **Safety & Productivity** (Weak)
   - Orders down 3% in Q3
   - Economic sensitivity
   - **Outlook**: Lower single digits, stabilizing

### Industrial Software Market

- **High Growth**: Double-digit ACV growth
- **Recurring Revenue**: Subscription-based model
- **High Margins**: Software margins significantly higher than hardware

**Current Performance:**
