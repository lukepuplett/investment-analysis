## Executive Summary

**BLUF: Emerson Electric demonstrates strong financial performance with **$17.78B TTM revenue**, **27.1% adjusted segment EBITA margin**, and **$2.74B free cash flow**, but faces headwinds from tariff exposure and FX impacts. The company shows **resilient growth** in process/hybrid markets and **discrete recovery**, with **strong balance sheet** ($2.22B cash, manageable debt) and **consistent shareholder returns**. **BUY** - Long-term investors seeking quality industrial automation exposure.**

Emerson Electric Co. is a global industrial technology leader providing advanced automation solutions. The company operates through two primary segments: Intelligent Devices and Software and Control. Q3 2025 results demonstrate resilient performance despite dynamic tariff environment, with strong profitability, healthy cash generation, and improving discrete market recovery.

**Key Financial Highlights:**
- Revenue (TTM): $17.78B, up 1.6% YoY
- Adjusted Segment EBITA Margin: 27.1% (consistent with prior year)
- Free Cash Flow (TTM): $2.74B, representing 15.4% margin
- Cash Position: $2.22B (MRQ)
- Dividend Yield: 1.51% with consistent payout

## Key Financial Trends

### Positive Trends
1. **Strong Profitability**: Consistent 27%+ adjusted segment EBITA margins
2. **Cash Generation**: Exceptional free cash flow generation ($2.74B TTM)
3. **Discrete Recovery**: Test & Measurement orders up 16%, discrete businesses turning positive
4. **Software Growth**: ACV at $1.5B, up 10% YoY, AspenTech digital grid management up 26%
5. **Operational Excellence**: Operating leverage of 25% (38% excluding tariffs)
6. **Backlog Strength**: $7.6B backlog (excluding AspenTech) supporting growth visibility

### Areas of Concern
1. **Tariff Exposure**: Despite mitigation, tariffs impacted margins by 40 bps in Q3
2. **FX Impact**: Unexpected FX headwind on Intelligent Devices margins (~110 bps)
3. **Leverage Increase**: Debt-to-equity increased to 71.5% post-AspenTech acquisition
4. **Europe Weakness**: European sales down 7% in Q3, reflecting economic headwinds
5. **Bulk Chemicals**: Continued weakness in Europe and China bulk chemical markets

## Conclusion

Emerson Electric demonstrates strong financial fundamentals with resilient profitability, exceptional cash generation, and improving growth dynamics. The company's strategic focus on industrial automation and software positions it well for long-term value creation. While near-term headwinds from tariffs and FX exist, the strong backlog, discrete recovery, and operational excellence support positive outlook. The balance sheet, while more leveraged post-AspenTech, remains manageable given strong cash flow generation. **Recommendation: BUY** for long-term investors seeking quality industrial automation exposure with consistent returns and growth potential.
