## Core Investment Rationale

### 1. Structural Secular Tailwind: AI Infrastructure Buildout

**The Opportunity:**
- Hyperscalers investing $50B+ annually in AI infrastructure buildout
- AI model training requires massive compute clusters with optical interconnects
- Data rates increasing exponentially (25x bandwidth increase for AI vs. traditional workloads)
- Optical interconnects becoming architectural requirement, not optional

**Corning's Dual Infrastructure Play:**

**Primary: Optical for Ground-Based Datacenters**
- Optical Communications segment growing 35% YoY ($6.3B revenue)
- Enterprise datacenter business growing 61% YoY (becoming primary driver)
- Gen AI products driving demand with 18-24 month competitive advantage
- Meta partnership validates market leadership

**Secondary (Emerging): Solar for Space-Based Datacenters**
- Space-based datacenters emerging as strategic infrastructure (latency optimization, redundancy)
- Corning building $2.5B solar business by 2028 (polysilicon + modules + specialized glass)
- Solar panels critical power infrastructure for orbital datacenters
- Positions Corning as dual-layer beneficiary of AI infrastructure expansion (ground optical + space solar)

**Multi-Year Visibility:**
- AI infrastructure buildout expected to continue 2026-2028 at elevated levels
- Data center lifespan of 15+ years creates long-term annuity-like revenue
- Inference scaling creating second wave of datacenter buildout 2027+
- Space-based infrastructure emerging opportunity 2027-2029 timeframe
- TAM expansion from $20B (2025) → $40-45B (2028) estimated, plus emerging space applications

**Durability Assessment:**
- Secular shift, not cyclical (AI becoming core technology foundation)
- No credible alternative technology available today
- Space infrastructure represents emerging TAM expansion vector
- Timing: Multi-year visibility through Springboard plan (2028), with emerging opportunities beyond

### 2. Springboard Plan: Doubling Revenue While Expanding Margins

**Original Plan (2023):**
- Add $8B incremental sales by 2028
- Sales run rate: $13.1B → $21B
- Operating margin target: 20% by 2026

**Upgraded Plan (Jan 2026):**
- Add $11B incremental sales by 2028 (38% increase to target)
- Sales run rate: $13.1B → $24.1B (84% growth vs. original 60%)
- Operating margin: 20% achieved in 2025 (1 year early!)
- 2026 high confidence plan: $5.75B incremental sales (up from $4B)

**Key Validations:**
- Early margin target achievement demonstrates execution excellence
- Upgrade reflects real visibility into demand (not just optimism)
- $11B incremental assumes $1-1.2B annual revenue growth 2026-2028 (realistic given 35% Optical growth)
- Operating margin maintenance at 20%+ despite 13%+ revenue growth shows operational leverage

**Margin Expansion Drivers:**
1. Mix shift toward higher-margin Optical Communications (16% margin, 35% growth)
2. Operating leverage (13% revenue growth, 26% op income growth)
3. Manufacturing efficiency improvements (scale-up benefits)
4. Pricing power in Gen AI products

### 3. Competitive Moat: Integrated Supply Chain + Product Leadership

**Integrated Advantage:**
- Fiber + Connectors + Assemblies as single supplier
- Competitors only compete at individual component level
- Corning's breadth creates switching costs and integration benefits
- No competitor has achieved similar integration (Prysmian = fiber only, Amphenol = connectors only)

**Product Leadership:**
- Gen AI products driving 61% enterprise growth
- Technology advantage: 18-24 month lag time for competitor replication
- Meta partnership validates product differentiation
- Patent portfolio of 500+ patents in optical interconnect space

**Customer Lock-in:**
- Architectural integration creates redesign costs (12-18 month cycle)
- Multi-year contracts provide revenue visibility
- First-mover advantage in hyperscaler datacenter designs
- Preferred vendor status with Meta, Google, Microsoft, Amazon (estimated)

**Moat Durability:**
- Integrated supply chain advantage: 8-10 years (persistent if Corning invests)
- Gen AI product advantage: 5-7 years (requires continued R&D)
- Customer relationships: 7-10 years (depends on product performance)
- Overall moat strength: 3.7/5 (Strong, defensible)

### 4. Financial Execution & Cash Generation

**FY2025 Results:**
- Revenue: $16.4B (+13% YoY)
- Operating margin: 19.3% (+180 bps), achieved 20% target (1 yr early!)
- Net income: $2.8B (+31% YoY)
- Free cash flow: $1.7B (+76% YoY, doubled from 2023)
- EPS: $2.52 (+22% YoY)

**Capital Allocation Discipline:**
- Organic growth investment prioritized (capex $283M = 1.7% of revenue)
- Customer financial support model sharing investment burden
- Share buyback program resumed (800M shares repurchased over decade)
- Dividend growth 19% CAGR over 5 years
- Long-term debt maturity: 21 years (strong balance sheet)

**Return on Capital:**
- ROE: 26.3% (exceptional)
- ROA: 9.6% (strong)
- ROIC: 14.2% (expanding)
- FCF yield: 1% (undervalued at current price)

### 5. Path to $24B Revenue by 2028 (Achievable)

**2025 Baseline:** $16.4B revenue
**Target:** $24.1B by end-2028
**Required Growth:** $7.7B incremental (47% growth over 3 years = 13% CAGR)

- Plan conservative given recent momentum (enterprise +61%, hyperscale "significantly faster")
