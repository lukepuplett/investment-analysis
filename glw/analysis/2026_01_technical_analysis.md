## Competing Technology Approaches

### Electrical Interconnects (Legacy)
**Status:** Reaching bandwidth limits
- Current max: 200-400G per connection
- Power consumption: 2-5W per 100G
- Latency: 5-10 nanoseconds

**Corning's Optical Advantage:**
- Bandwidth: 400G-1.6Tbps+ (10x+ higher)
- Power: 0.1-0.5W per 100G (20-50x lower)
- Latency: <1 nanosecond (5-10x lower)
- Conclusion: Optical becoming mandatory, not optional, for AI datacenters

### Photonics-Based Solutions (Emerging)
**Status:** Early-stage research, 5-10 year horizon
- Co-packaged optics (CPO) embedding optics in silicon
- Potential to reduce size/cost in long-term
- Challenges: Manufacturing scale, thermal management, cost

**Corning's Position:**
- Potential acquisition target for photonic IP if needed
- Optical fiber complementary to photonic solutions (interconnect layer)
- Timeline provides opportunity to invest and monitor

### Wireless Interconnect (Speculative)
**Status:** Not viable for datacenters
- Insufficient bandwidth for direct server connections
- Electromagnetic interference in dense environments
- Latency unpredictable for AI workloads

**Assessment:** Not credible threat to optical dominance in datacenters.

## Supply Chain & Raw Materials

### Fiber Production Inputs
1. **Silica (SiO2):** Primary material, abundant

   - Cost: $5-10/kg (minor cost component)

2. **Rare Earth Elements (Erbium, Ytterbium):** For fiber amplification
