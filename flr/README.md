## Investment Thesis

### Executive Summary (BLUF)

**Fluor represents a compelling value opportunity in infrastructure and energy transition markets with strong balance sheet protection ($2.3B cash, low debt) and substantial backlog ($28.2B, 80% reimbursable) providing revenue visibility.** Current pullback to $46.32 (-3.60% today, Nov 4, 2025) improves risk-reward profile ahead of Q3 earnings (Nov 7, 2025). The company faces near-term headwinds from infrastructure project execution issues and client hesitancy due to trade uncertainty, but maintains leading positions in LNG and mining EPC markets with strong long-term secular tailwinds.

**However, investors must understand: EPC contracting is an inherently risky business model.** Projects almost always overrun due to complexity, long timelines, subcontractor risk, and third-party dependencies. Even reimbursable contracts (80% of backlog) aren't fully protective - they often have GMP caps, require funding costs upfront before recovery, and face disputes over recoverability. This is why EPC contractors trade at discounted valuations (P/S 0.3-0.8x) and require strong balance sheets.

**Risk-reward profile is balanced with strong downside protection but requires tolerance for execution risk and earnings volatility.**

### Core Investment Case

**1. Strong Balance Sheet Protection**
- $2.3B cash provides significant cushion for overruns and working capital needs
- Low debt ($1.07B) with conservative leverage (17.9% debt-to-equity)
- Strong liquidity position (current ratio 1.62)
- Critical for managing inherent EPC business model volatility

**2. Substantial Backlog with Favorable Risk Profile**
- $28.2B backlog provides ~1.7 years of revenue visibility
- 80% reimbursable reduces fixed-price risk exposure (though not fully protective)
- Diversified across Urban Solutions (73%), Energy Solutions (20%), Mission Solutions (7%)
- Long-term contracts with established clients provide stability

**3. Leading Market Positions in Attractive Sectors**
- **LNG EPC:** Leading position (~15-20% market share) with proven execution (LNG Canada Train 1 complete, Phase 2 FEED awarded)
- **Mining EPC:** Leading position (~10-15% market share) with strong relationships (BHP, Rio Tinto, Freeport-McMoRan)
- **Infrastructure:** Competitive position with large project execution capability
- **Data Centers:** Emerging position with relationship development

**4. Exposure to Strong Secular Trends**
- Energy transition: Mining demand for critical minerals (copper, lithium, rare earths)
- Infrastructure investment: Government support and infrastructure spending needs
- Reshoring: U.S. manufacturing and mining development
- AI infrastructure: Data center construction boom
- Energy security: LNG export and nuclear power expansion

**5. Recovery Potential**
- Infrastructure projects completing: Gordie Howe (97%), 635/LBJ (78%), I-35 Phase 2 (58%)
- Legacy project funding declining: $200M/year expected to decline to $0 by 2029
- Trade resolution: Client hesitancy expected to resolve once trade policy stabilizes
- Execution improvement: Mitigation efforts underway on infrastructure projects

### Key Investment Risks

**1. Execution Risk (HIGH)**
- Three infrastructure projects experiencing cost overruns ($54M Q2 impact)
- EPC projects inherently prone to overruns due to complexity, long timelines, subcontractor issues
- Even reimbursable contracts have GMP caps and require funding costs upfront
- Requires close monitoring and execution improvement

**2. Market Risk (MODERATE-HIGH)**
- Client hesitancy due to trade uncertainty delaying project decisions
- Project cancellations and extended deferrals
- Soft markets in chemicals and batteries
- Reduced bookings and slower growth potential

**3. Cash Flow Risk (LOW-MODERATE)**
- Cash flow volatility: Negative operating cash flow in Q2 (-$21M)
- Working capital increases on large projects
- Legacy project funding ($200M+ annually, declining over time)
- Funding costs upfront, recovering through claims/disputes later

**4. Fundamental Business Model Risk**
- EPC contracting is inherently risky - projects almost always overrun
- Reimbursable contracts help but don't eliminate risk (GMP caps, disputes, timing)
- This is why valuations are discounted and balance sheets must be strong
- Requires risk tolerance and long-term perspective
