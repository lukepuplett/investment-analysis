## Executive Summary

**BLUF: Fluor Corporation shows mixed financial performance with strong balance sheet fundamentals ($2.3B cash, low debt) but faces near-term headwinds from infrastructure project cost issues and client hesitancy. Q2 2025 adjusted EBITDA declined 42% YoY to $96M, but the company maintains a robust $28.2B backlog (80% reimbursable) providing visibility. Strong recovery potential exists once trade uncertainty resolves and infrastructure projects complete. Hold recommendation - value investors seeking infrastructure exposure with execution risk tolerance.**

## Investment Implications

### Positive Factors
- Strong balance sheet provides cushion for operations and capital allocation
- Large backlog provides revenue visibility
- Reimbursable mix reduces risk exposure
- Recovery potential once infrastructure projects complete and trade uncertainty resolves

### Risk Factors
- Near-term margin pressure from infrastructure project issues
- Cash flow volatility and working capital management challenges
- Client hesitancy impacting bookings and growth trajectory
- Execution risk on remaining infrastructure projects

### Recommendation
**Hold for value investors with execution risk tolerance.** The company has strong fundamentals but faces near-term headwinds. Recovery potential exists once infrastructure projects complete and trade uncertainty resolves, but requires patience and risk tolerance for execution challenges.
