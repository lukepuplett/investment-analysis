## Executive Summary

**BLUF: Fluor faces moderate to high risk levels driven by execution challenges on infrastructure projects, cyclical market headwinds from trade uncertainty, and cash flow volatility. However, the company's strong balance sheet ($2.3B cash), low debt ($1.07B), and 80% reimbursable backlog provide significant risk mitigation. Key risks include infrastructure project execution, client hesitancy from trade uncertainty, and legacy project funding. Risk-reward profile is balanced with strong downside protection but execution risks requiring monitoring. Hold recommendation - risk-tolerant investors seeking infrastructure exposure with strong balance sheet protection.**

## Risk Categories

### 1. Execution Risk

**Risk Level: High**

**Description:** Project execution challenges leading to cost overruns and schedule delays

**Evidence:**
- Three infrastructure projects experiencing cost growth ($54M net impact in Q2)
- Gordie Howe Bridge: Cost increases due to rework and handover complexities
- 635/LBJ: Material cost escalation and labor productivity impacts
- I-35 Phase 2: Subcontractor default, utility delays, mitigation costs

**Impact:**
- Financial: Margin compression, earnings impact
- Reputation: Client confidence, competitive position
- Operational: Resource allocation, management focus

**Mitigation:**
- Increased oversight and strengthened execution teams
- Subcontractor management and recovery actions
- Process improvements and lessons learned
- Selective project pursuit with proven pursuit principles

**Probability:** Moderate (recent occurrence, mitigation efforts underway)  
**Severity:** High (significant financial impact)  
**Overall Risk:** **High** - Requires close monitoring and successful mitigation

### 2. Market and Economic Risk

**Risk Level: Moderate to High**

**Description:** Cyclical market headwinds from trade uncertainty, cost escalation, and interest rates

**Evidence:**
- Client hesitancy due to trade policy uncertainty
- Project cancellations and extended deferrals
- Reduced CapEx budgets in chemicals and battery markets
- Cost escalation from material and labor inflation

**Impact:**
- Financial: Reduced bookings, slower revenue growth
- Operational: Project delays, market softness
- Strategic: Growth trajectory, market positioning

**Mitigation:**
- Diversified portfolio across multiple end markets
- Strong backlog provides revenue visibility ($28.2B)
- Policy support expected to materialize
- Focus on high-growth markets (mining, data centers, LNG)

**Probability:** Moderate to High (current conditions, potential resolution)  
**Severity:** Moderate (impact on growth, timing)  
**Overall Risk:** **Moderate to High** - Dependent on trade policy resolution timing
