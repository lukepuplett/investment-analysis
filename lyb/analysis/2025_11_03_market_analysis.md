## Executive Summary

**BLUF: LYB operates in a global petrochemical industry experiencing its longest downturn in decades, with capacity rationalization accelerating across Asia and Europe. Mature markets showing early recovery signs with U.S. PE volumes rebounding and European demand up 3% YTD, while emerging markets drive long-term growth. Global capacity closures totaling ~30M tons (10% of supply) are rebalancing markets, with China's anti-involution policies and regional shutdowns creating constructive mid-term outlook. Company positioned in cost-advantaged North America and Middle East regions with 70%+ capacity share expected by next decade. Market normalization expected but timing uncertain; current trough valuations reflect deep cyclical pressure.**

The global petrochemical industry is navigating the most prolonged downturn in management's 35-year experience, driven by overcapacity, weak demand, and economic headwinds. However, accelerating capacity rationalization across Asia and Europe, combined with resilient underlying demand fundamentals, suggests the market is approaching an inflection point. LyondellBasell's strategic positioning in cost-advantaged regions and focus on circular economy solutions positions it well for the eventual recovery.

## Global Market Overview

### Industry Size and Structure
- **Global Polyethylene Market:** ~$200B+ annually
- **Global Polyolefins Market:** ~$400B+ annually
- **Industry Structure:** Highly cyclical, capital-intensive, commodity-driven
- **Key End Markets:** Packaging (largest), automotive, construction, consumer goods

### Current Market Conditions
- **Status:** Deep cyclical trough, longest downturn in decades
- **Demand:** Weak across most regions and end markets
- **Supply:** Overcapacity particularly in China and Asia
- **Pricing:** Depressed margins across most product categories
- **Operating Rates:** Below historical averages globally

## Regional Market Dynamics

### North America
**Market Position:** Structural cost advantages with low-cost NGL feedstocks

**Current Conditions:**
- U.S. domestic polyethylene sales volumes beginning to rebound after downturn that began in 2022
- Q2 2025 domestic sales volumes highest since Q2 2022
- Producer inventories declining by 3 days of sales in Q2
- Export demand improving as tariff uncertainties resolved
- Operating rates remaining high (~85-90% for crackers)

**Key Drivers:**
- Feedstock cost advantage: Access to low-cost natural gas liquids
- Infrastructure: Well-developed pipeline and export infrastructure
- Market Position: Strong domestic market position with export capability
- Outlook: Expected to outperform global average through cycle

**Competitive Position:**
- LYB has strong North American position
- Export capability essential for balancing markets
- Hurricane season providing support for pricing

### Europe
**Market Position:** High-cost region facing structural challenges

**Current Conditions:**
- Polyethylene demand recovering with August YTD volumes up 3% vs last year
- Weak industrial and consumer demand expected to persist
- Increased competition from imports pressuring pricing
- High feedstock and energy costs challenging competitiveness
- Over 20% of European ethylene capacity closures announced since 2020

**Key Drivers:**
- Regulatory Burden: High compliance costs and environmental regulations
- Energy Costs: Structurally high energy costs vs other regions
- Capacity Rationalization: Significant closures improving supply/demand balance
- Circular Economy: Growing focus on recycled and renewable feedstocks

**Competitive Position:**
- LYB selling four European assets to AEQUITA
- Focusing on circular economy solutions (MoReTec-1)
- Reducing exposure to structurally disadvantaged region

### Asia (China Focus)
**Market Position:** Oversupplied with rationalization accelerating

**Current Conditions:**
- Rapid capacity growth creating significant overcapacity
- Chinese polyethylene not globally competitive due to high costs
- Operating rates falling as capacity grows
- NDRC anti-involution policies expected to rationalize older assets

**Key Drivers:**
- Capacity Growth: Massive new capacity additions in recent years
- Government Policy: Anti-involution measures targeting overcapacity
- Market Structure: Many non-integrated, non-flexible, older crackers
- Rationalization: Expected closures of least competitive assets

**Rationalization Progress:**
- 6.2M tons of closures already underway
- 5.0M tons announced closures
- ~10.1M tons anticipated closures
- Total ~21.3M tons expected rationalization

**Competitive Position:**
- LYB maintains light asset footprint in China
- Focus on technical and commercial presence
- Joint ventures running at minimum technical rates

### Middle East
**Market Position:** Cost-advantaged region with competitive feedstocks

**Key Drivers:**
- Low-cost feedstock access
- Energy cost advantages
- Strategic location for global markets
- Growing capacity share expected

**Competitive Position:**
- LYB has joint ventures in Saudi Arabia (NATPET with Alujain)
- Potential for further expansion
- Strategic positioning in cost-advantaged region

- **Essential Products:** Reinforces essential role of polyolefins in everyday applications

**Key Drivers:**

- Rising middle class in emerging markets
- Food safety and hygiene requirements

**Outlook:** Stable growth driven by demographic trends and essential nature of products

### Building & Construction
**Market Size:** Significant end market for polyolefins

**Current Conditions:**

### Automotive
**Market Size:** Important end market for advanced polymers

**Current Conditions:**
- **Production:** Forecasts less pessimistic as trade agreements provide clarity
- **Demand:** Seasonal shutdowns impacting Q3 production
- **Tariffs:** Volatility creating ongoing uncertainty
- **Trends:** EV mobility driving demand for specialized polymers

**Key Drivers:**
- Vehicle production volumes
- Trade policy and tariffs
- EV adoption and electrification
- Lightweighting trends

**Outlook:** Improving outlook as trade clarity improves and EV adoption accelerates

### Fuels (Oxyfuels)
**Market Size:** Niche but important market

**Current Conditions:**
- **Margins:** Remaining low despite summer driving season
- **Supply:** Oversupplied market with capacity additions
- **Seasonality:** Typical compression in gasoline crack spreads
- **Competition:** Additional volume from China displacing U.S. Gulf Coast volumes

**Key Drivers:**
- Crude oil prices
- Gasoline crack spreads
- Octane blend premiums
- Regional supply/demand balances

**Outlook:** Challenging near-term but structural support from octane requirements

## Capacity Rationalization Trends

### Global Capacity Closures
**Total Expected Closures:** ~30M+ tons through 2028 (~10% of current global supply)

**By Region:**
- **Southeast Asia:** 2.9M tons (closures to date)
- **China:** ~21.3M tons (6.2M closed, 5.0M announced, ~10.1M anticipated)
- **Europe:** ~3.5M tons (anticipated closures)
- **Japan:** ~2.7-3.7M tons (announced closures)
- **South Korea:** ~2.7-3.7M tons (announced closures)

**Timeline:**
- 30% of closures announced in past 12 months
- Acceleration of rationalization across multiple regions
- Domino effect helping offset new supply from China

### Impact on Market Balance
- **Supply Reduction:** Significant capacity closures improving balances
- **Regional Focus:** Closures concentrated in high-cost regions
- **Market Rebalancing:** Creating more constructive mid-term outlook
- **Timing:** Expected to take several years to fully impact markets

### Global Polyethylene Demand Growth

- 1990: Index 100
- 2025F: Index 450
- 2030F: Index 500
- Long-term CAGR: ~3-4% globally

### Demand Drivers
1. **Population Growth:** Rising global population
2. **Urbanization:** Increasing urban populations globally
3. **Rising Middle Class:** Improved living standards in emerging markets
4. **Infrastructure:** Investments in energy, digitalization, infrastructure
5. **Circular Economy:** Innovation driving new applications

### Mature Markets vs Emerging Markets
**Mature Markets (North America, Europe, Japan):**
- High per-capita consumption
- Growth driven by innovation, efficiency, circularity
- Stable demand patterns
- Focus on infrastructure, electrification, EV mobility

**Emerging Markets (India, Africa, Southeast Asia):**
- Low per-capita consumption but rapid growth
- Rising populations and improved living standards
- Significant growth potential
- Long-term growth drivers

### Market Opportunities
- **Recycled Polymers:** Growing demand for recycled content
- **Chemical Recycling:** Advanced recycling technologies (MoReTec)
