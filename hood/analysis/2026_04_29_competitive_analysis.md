# Robinhood Markets, Inc. (HOOD) — Competitive analysis

**As of:** April 29, 2026  
**Sources:** Internal notes; [Q1 FY2026 transcript](../quarterly/2026_Q1_earnings_call_transcript.txt). **Peers not re-priced in-repo — refresh Yahoo or filings for peer comps.**

## BLUF

**What this note is:** who Robinhood is really racing—and what is (and isn’t) defensible. **Main takeaway:** **Zero commissions** is table stakes now; everyone serious matches or beats on price. Where firms fight is **breadth** (cash, investing, options, futures, prediction markets, crypto, banking/card), **how the app feels**, **trust after past crises**, **reliability of execution**, and **winning customer cash and deposits**. Big incumbents (Fidelity-, Schwab-style) and crypto-native apps are all in the mix. Robinhood is **differentiated**, not untouchable: moat is **brand + product stack + licenses**, not a monopoly—see the scorecard below for detail.

## Competitive Moat Scorecard

| Moat factor | Rating (1–5) | Notes | Durability horizon | Replicability |
|-------------|:---:|:------|--------------------|----------------|
| Scale / retail brand | 4 | National retail recognition; episodic reputational setbacks historically | Medium–long | High with capital |
| Zero-commission parity | 2 | Industry-matched minimum; differentiation moved to product | Short | Absolute |
| Product breadth / integrated cash & trade | 4 | Banking + brokerage + derivatives + subscriptions | Medium | Medium |
| Regulatory licenses & clearance stack | 4 | Barriers vs unregulated entrants; costly to replicate | Long | Medium |
| Technology & velocity (shipping cadence / AI-assisted dev) | 4 | CFO cites tooling adoption and engineering throughput metrics | Medium | Medium-high |
| Proprietary liquidity / vertically integrated venues | 3–4 | Prediction-exchange JV (**Rothera**) narrative lifts control | Medium | High for capital-heavy peers |

_Unweighted illustrative average lands **mid-3s** — suggestive of **differentiation**, not classical wide-moat oligopoly._
