## Executive Thesis (1-page)

HOOD has transformed from a loss-making retail broker (2022–23) to a high-margin profitability story ($1.88B net income TTM, 46% operating margins). The core business—**retail deposit growth + trading + subscriptions**—is real and validating quarter-over-quarter. But at **37× forward P/E**, the market is pricing **perfection**: 15%+ revenue growth AND margin stability AND regulatory benignity must all hold simultaneously.

**Realistic view:**
- **Bull case (25% probability):** Deposits scale to $100B+, Gold subs reach 6M+, event/prediction markets launch unchallenged, international boots-on-ground succeed. FY2027 revenue $6.2B, net income $2.85B. Fair value **$75–85B**. Current price offers **no margin of safety**.
- **Base case (50% probability):** Moderate 13% revenue growth ($5.4B), margins compress 100–150bps due to OpEx inflation (Trump Accounts, international), regulatory settlements cost $200–400M revenue. FY2027 net income $2.16B. Fair value **$48–55B**. Current price is **33% overvalued**.
- **Bear case (25% probability):** Event contracts face regulatory bans, PFOF restrictions hit, retail churn accelerates, card losses spike. Revenue stalls ($4.6B, −8% YoY), margins collapse to 40–42%. FY2027 net income $1.8B. Fair value **$25–35B**. Downside **45–65%**.

**Valuation implication:** HOOD is expensive relative to fundamentals unless growth persists. The stock reprices fast on setbacks (high beta 2.5). Better entry at **$59B** (near base-case fair value); current price rewards patient buyers only if bull thesis materializes.
