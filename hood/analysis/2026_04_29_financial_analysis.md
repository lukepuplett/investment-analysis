**Sources:** [yahoo_stats](../financials/2026_04/yahoo_stats.md), [income_statement](../financials/2026_04/income_statement.md), [balance_sheet](../financials/2026_04/balance_sheet.md), [cashflow](../financials/2026_04/cashflow.md), [2026_Q1_10-Q](../quarterly/2026_Q1_10q.txt), [Q1 FY2026 earnings call transcript](../quarterly/2026_Q1_earnings_call_transcript.txt).

## BLUF

**What this note is:** the numbers-first check on whether Robinhood earns money at scale—and how noisy the cash picture is. **Main takeaway:** the business flipped from big **annual losses** a few years ago to **meaningful profitability** on roughly **$4.47B** trailing revenue and strong operating income (**~$2.09B**) and net income (**~$1.88B**) using the pasted Yahoo annual grid (**$ thousands**). **Latest quarter filed:** ~**$1,067M** revenue in Q1 2026 versus **~$927M** a year earlier (~**15%** growth)—aligned with management’s Q1 commentary. Operating cash looks healthy on headline TTM (**~$1.64B OCF**, **~$1.58B** free cash flow lines in pasted stats), **but** brokerage businesses move large customer cash and clearing balances **through** the statements: **never read “cash flow” here without filing context.** Stock charts and sentiment are **not** the same topic as GAAP revenue (see [market_analysis](./2026_04_29_market_analysis.md) for HOOD-specific price framing).
