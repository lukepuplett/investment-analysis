## Key Investment Considerations

### Strengths (Q1 2026)
- **Record backlog & customer diversification:** $99.4B backlog (+78% since Q3 2025), 10 customers at $1B+ each, <30% from non-investment-grade labs. 4 of world’s leading AI model developers (OpenAI, Anthropic, Meta, Google) now customers; 9 of 10 AI leaders outside China.
- **Inference monetization now >50% of workload:** Pricing power validated across all GPU generations (A100, H100, H200, L40S) with QoQ increases. Inference = durable, margin-accretive revenue stream.
- **New enterprise verticals emerging at scale:** Financial services ~$10B backlog (Jane Street, Hudson River Trading), Physical AI >$1B (robotics, autonomous driving, scientific discovery). Inference acceleration = real-world productivity gains driving broad adoption.
- **Capital structure breakthrough:** Investment-grade debt access (<6% cost via DDTL 4.0, first ever HPC-backed), non-recourse to parent, oversubscribed. $20B+ debt/equity secured YTD at declining rates. No debt maturities until 2029. Access to capital now hyperscaler-competitive.
- **Execution & scale:** 1.1 GW active power (hyperscale milestone), 50 data centers across diversified providers (no single provider >17% of capacity), 3.5 GW contracted, dock-to-live times industry-leading. Q1 trough operating margin (1%) is known, timing-based; sequential expansion expected through year-end.
- **2027 visibility:** >$30B ARR target with 75% pre-contracted (excluding renewals). Ample power capacity already secured to deliver 2027 targets.

### Risks (Q1 2026)
- **Component cost inflation & CapEx escalation:** CapEx guidance raised to $31–35B FY2026 due to higher component pricing (GPUs, memory, power modules). While management passes through costs in new contracts, prior contracts with locked-in pricing could face margin squeeze on components.
- **Margin timing & execution cadence:** Q1 margin compression to 1% operating margin reflects rapid capacity ramp (tripled active power YoY). "Powered shell → revenue" lag (1–2 month fit-out) creates temporary negative contribution margins. Execution at 50 data centers across multiple geographies/partners introduces coordination risk.
- **Interest expense growth:** Q1 interest expense $536M (doubled YoY) as debt scales. Even at <6% cost, $20B+ debt balance generates $1B+ annual interest. Must validate debt decline as capex moderates post-2026.
- **Hyperscaler competition:** AWS, Azure, Google Cloud could disrupt via proprietary capacity or pricing. OpenAI/Meta ownership of CoreWeave capacity introduces capex discipline risk if internal capacity strategies shift.
- **Supply chain concentration:** Despite 50 data centers, GPU supply (NVIDIA-dependent) and component availability remain potential bottlenecks. Tariffs or trade restrictions could escalate costs further.
- **Demand sustainability:** Backlog is 5-year contracted, but commitment from foundation labs/hyperscalers could tighten if AI capex cycles shift or training efficiency improves (requiring fewer GPUs).
