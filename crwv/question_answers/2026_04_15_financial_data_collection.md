## Key Trends & Strategic Positioning

### ✅ **Strength 1: Insatiable Demand**
- Pricing remained stable throughout 2025 despite tight GPU supply
- Demand accelerating from hyperscalers, AI natives, and enterprises
- "Largely all 2026 capacity allocated" – supply constraint remains
- Inference workloads proliferating; H100 and A100 pricing holding firm

### ✅ **Strength 2: Execution at Scale**
- Delivered 260 MW in Q4 – largest quarterly addition to date
- Cleared Q3 data center delays and deployed 50,000+ Grace Blackwell GPUs in weeks
- First cloud to reach NVIDIA Exemplar Cloud status for GB200
- SemiAnalysis' sole Platinum-ranked AI cloud provider

### ✅ **Strength 3: Fortress Backlog & Visibility**
- $66.8B contracted backlog = 13x annual revenue, 2+ years visibility
- 3.1 GW contracted power, 93% online by end-2027
- Recurring revenue model via reserved instance contracts
- Cash flow visibility strong despite current loss position

### ⚠️ **Risk 1: Massive CapEx Requirements**
- 2026 CapEx guidance: $30B+ (2.6x forecasted revenue)
- Cumulative CapEx to build out contracted capacity: estimated $50B+ through 2027
- Near-term margins deeply negative (-82% operating margin TTM)
- Dependent on access to capital and sustained demand

### ⚠️ **Risk 2: Customer Concentration**
- Largest customers likely hyperscalers (though CEO emphasized diversification)
- Weighted toward GPU/AI workloads; limited revenue diversification
- Macro/AI cycle downturn could pressure utilization and pricing

### ⚠️ **Risk 3: Competitive Intensity**
- Established cloud providers (AWS, Azure, GCP) expanding GPU offerings
- Emerging competitors: Lambda Labs, Crusoe, other GPU cloud plays
- Proprietary software/stack (SKI, Mission Control) key differentiation but unproven at scale
