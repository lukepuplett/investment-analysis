**Date:** May 6, 2026

## Executive Summary

**BLUF: Siemens Energy operates mature, proven technologies across its portfolio with proven track records. Core competency lies in power generation equipment (heavy-duty gas turbines, synchronous condensers, power modules) and grid infrastructure (transformers, FACTS devices). Technical risks are moderate: technologies are field-proven, but execution on new products (SG 7.0 wind turbine, modular power systems for data centers) remains unproven at scale. Key technical opportunities center on next-generation platforms: SG 7.0 narrowbody turbine (first order Q1 2026, launch target 2027), modular power systems for data centers (22 GW backlog, revenue scaling 2026-2028), and SMR (Small Modular Reactors) for future grid decarbonization (timeline 2030+, early stage). Technology differentiation is strongest in grid stabilization (synchronous condensers, FACTS) where Siemens has proven expertise and limited competition. Technical execution risk is real but manageable given established manufacturing and field service networks.**

### 1. Heavy-Duty Gas Turbines (Core Business)

#### Technology Overview

- **Efficiency:** 50%+ electrical efficiency (modern designs); target 60%+ with advanced cooling
- **Fuel Flexibility:** Natural gas primary; dual-fuel variants available (gas/oil)
- **Reliability:** >95% availability in service

#### Technical Capabilities (Q1 FY26 Evidence)

**Q1 Order Intake Analysis:**
- **102 gas turbines booked** in Q1 = strong demand across all frame sizes
- Management: "Momentum was broad across all turbine frames" (no concentration on low-end models)
- **13 GW new capacity** = typical utility-scale ordering patterns (healthy mix)

**Manufacturing Scale:**
- Production facilities in Germany, Mexico, US
- Q1 production rate: ~102 units suggests run-rate of 400+ units/year capacity
- Supply chain: integrated (own components); outsource specialty items (castings, compressor blades)

**Service Base (LTSA):**
- Management: "Gas Services delivered its strongest quarter ever in terms of order intake"
- LTSA contracts provide 20-30 year revenue visibility
- Aftermarket margin: 70%+ gross margin (vs. 40-50% on OEM sales)

### 4. Grid Technologies: Synchronous Condensers & FACTS

#### Technology Overview
**Synchronous Condensers:**
- Electrical machines that provide grid stability and reactive power support
- Critical for maintaining frequency as coal plants retire and renewable generation scales
- Proven technology; Siemens has decades of experience
- Recent outages (Berlin, US winter storms) driving sudden capex allocation

**FACTS (Flexible AC Transmission Systems):**
- Power electronics devices to dynamically control power flow on transmission lines
- Examples: Static VAR Compensators (SVC), Static Synchronous Compensators (STATCOM)
- Enable grid to accommodate renewable variability without new transmission lines
- Siemens has proven track record; market expanding rapidly

#### Q1 FY26 Market Evidence

**Synchronous Condenser Demand:**
- Management: "Recent events raise awareness and increase demand for grid stabilization technologies like our synchronous condensers"
- Berlin cable sabotage (Feb 2026) = proof point of grid criticality
- US winter storms = political pressure for grid resilience

**FACTS Orders (Q1):**
- Management: "Large FACTS orders with a total amount of a low triple-digit million EUR value"
- Implies €100-300M FACTS orders in Q1 (significant volume)
- Grid stabilization = emerging TAM as renewable penetration increases

#### Technical Capabilities & Differentiation

**Siemens Advantages:**
1. **Proven Track Record:** Decades of synchronous condenser installations globally
2. **Manufacturing Expertise:** Wollsdorf facility (Austria) supplies both synchronous condensers and transformers
3. **Grid Integration Knowledge:** Understanding of grid dynamics, frequency control, stability
4. **Digital Integration:** AI-enabled controls for predictive maintenance and grid optimization

**Competitive Position:**
- Siemens is market leader in synchronous condensers
- ABB, Mitsubishi competitive but smaller presence
- Chinese competitors emerging but less proven in grid stability applications

#### Timeline to Massive TAM Expansion

| Driver                          | Timeline | Impact                              |
|--------------------------------|----------|-------------------------------------|
| **Coal Plant Retirements (EU, US)**| 2026-2030| +500 GW synchronous condenser need |
| **Renewable Integration (Grid Codes)**| 2026+ | Mandatory grid stabilization capex |
| **Frequency Events (Political Pressure)**| 2026+ | Acceleration of investment decisions |
| **Offshore Wind Integration**   | 2026-2028| Submarine cables requiring converters + synchronous condensers |

#### Assessment: **STRONG COMPETITIVE ADVANTAGE** (8/10 for differentiation)

**Technical Status:** Proven, mature technology. Siemens is global leader. TAM expanding rapidly due to renewable integration.

**Upside:** If EU mandates synchronous condensers for renewable integration (likely by 2027), TAM could expand 3-5x. Siemens positioned to capture significant share.

### 5. Long-Term Technologies: SMR (Small Modular Reactors) & Hydrogen

#### SMR (Small Modular Reactors)

**Technology Overview:**
- Modular nuclear reactors (50-300 MW units) for distributed power generation
- Proposed for remote locations, industrial heat, grid stability
- Reduces capital risk vs. traditional large reactors
- Carbon-free baseload power for energy transition

**Siemens Position:**
- Partner in SMR ventures (via industrial partnerships, not core business)
- Not primary OEM; provides components/systems
- Limited disclosure in Q1 results

**Timeline:**
- Target: 2030-2035 for first commercial deployments
- High regulatory uncertainty; not near-term driver

**Assessment:** Long-term optionality, not material to near-term strategy.

#### Hydrogen Turbines

**Technology Overview:**
- Gas turbines modified to burn hydrogen fuel instead of natural gas
- Path to 100% carbon-free power generation
- Pre-commercial prototypes underway globally
- Hydrogen supply chain still nascent

**Siemens Position:**
- R&D underway; GE, Mitsubishi further along
- No commercial units yet
- Timeline: 2030+ target

**Risk:** Hydrogen infrastructure (production, transport, storage) is bottleneck. Turbine technology is less critical than fuel supply chain.

**Assessment:** Strategic hedge, but not near-term growth driver.

## Technology Assessment Summary

**Strengths:**
1. **Proven Core Portfolio:** Heavy-duty turbines, transformers, grid stabilization = mature, field-proven technologies
2. **Market-Driven Development:** New products (SG 7.0, modular power) aligned with customer needs (narrowbody retrofits, data center power)
3. **Supply Chain Resilience:** ASTA partnership and regional manufacturing expansion de-risk supply
4. **Grid Stabilization Leadership:** Synchronous condensers and FACTS position Siemens as leader in fastest-growing grid segment

**Weaknesses:**
1. **Late to Market (SG 7.0):** Vestas, GE already shipping 7+ MW turbines; Siemens 2-3 years late
2. **Unproven at Scale (Modular Power):** 22 GW backlog is impressive, but production at scale still unproven
3. **Hydrogen/SMR Behind:** GE, other competitors further along on hydrogen turbines; SMR still speculative
4. **Gamesa Drag:** Onshore wind segment commoditized; SG 7.0 is catch-up play, not differentiation

**Execution Risks:**
- SG 7.0 certification/cost parity delays
- Modular data center power production ramps slower than forecast
- Customer vertical integration (hyperscalers building own power systems)
- Supply chain constraints (copper, semiconductors, specialty materials)

**Overall Technical Assessment: SOLID WITH UPSIDE OPTIONALITY** (7/10)

Siemens has strong foundation in proven technologies (gas turbines, grid infrastructure) with manageable technical risks. Growth drivers (SG 7.0, modular power systems) are real but execution-dependent. Biggest technical risk is SG 7.0 launch timing and cost competitiveness; if successful, could drive significant incremental revenue. Modular data center power is emerging high-growth opportunity if hyperscaler adoption accelerates. Long-term bets (hydrogen, SMR) are strategic hedges, not material to 2026-2028 outlook.
