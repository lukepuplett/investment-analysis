# Photronics, Inc. (PLAB)

## Company overview

Photronics manufactures **photomasks** (reticles) used in semiconductor **IC** fabrication and **flat-panel display (FPD)** production. Customers include foundries, IDMs, and display makers; management emphasizes **high-end** IC layers, **regionalization** of fab supply chains, and **merchant** sourcing versus captive mask shops.

**Ticker:** PLAB  
**Exchange:** NASDAQ  
**Sector / industry:** Semiconductors — photomask manufacturing (merchant supplier)

## Competition (call + industry context)

**On the call:** Management does **not** name competitors; they describe **Chinese newcomers** focused on **mainstream** nodes, while **high-end** often has **only one or two qualified suppliers** per customer because qualification is expensive in fab resources.

**Typical peer set (merchant / global):** **Toppan Photomask**, **Dai Nippon Printing (DNP)**, **HOYA**, plus regional players such as **Taiwan Mask**, **SK-Electronics**, **LG Innotek** (display-adjacent), **Compugraphics** (Europe / quick-turn niche), and **domestic China mask vendors**. **Captive** fab mask capacity competes indirectly; management highlights **outsourcing from captives** as a tailwind.

**Market share:** Photronics is **not** disclosed on the call as a % of global wafers. Third-party reports **disagree** by definition (IC vs FPD, merchant vs captive, masks vs blanks). Fair qualitative label: **among the largest merchant photomask vendors**, often grouped with **Toppan** and **DNP** in revenue league tables—take numeric share from filings/models with caution.

## Leading-edge / nm exposure (from call)

Not exhaustive, but management cited:

- **Logic:** HVM around **12 nm / 14 nm**; **qualifications** toward **8 nm and below**
- **DRAM:** Qual activity for patterns **below 20 nm** using **multi-beam** writers
- **R&D:** Masks for **High-NA EUV** development; **AI-related advanced packaging**
- **China demand:** Strong **22–28 nm** tape-out activity (node migration vs prior years)

Photomask “node” is **per layer / technology**, not a single company-wide label.

## Growth themes (from call)

- **High-end IC** demand (record high-end revenue this quarter)
- **Regionalization / geopolitics** → duplicate tape-outs across fabs/regions
- **China:** migration to **higher nodes**; company emphasizes **high-end** positioning in Xiamen
- **U.S.:** **Allen, TX** expansion (tools installing; customer quals targeted **2H 2026**); **mainstream** US fab demand and freeing **Boise** for high-end
- **Boise:** **multi-beam** qualifications and CapEx for **high-end capacity**
- **FPD:** **AMOLED** complexity; **G8.6** early stage; new **advanced mask writer** in Korea

## Bottlenecks and risks (from call + Q&A)

- **Capacity:** **High-end Boise** described as slightly **capacity-constrained** vs unlimited demand; ramp depends on tools and **customer qualifications**
- **Visibility:** **Short backlog**; **few high-ASP orders** can move revenue and EPS materially
- **Seasonality:** **Chinese New Year** affects output (explicit in Q2 guide)
- **CapEx / depreciation:** **Elevated FY2026 spend**; margins may fluctuate with **mix** and depreciation; management argued they do **not** expect margins to “fall off a cliff”
- **China mainstream:** Potential **pricing pressure** as locals scale; strategy is **mix up** to high-end
- **JV cash:** Substantial cash sits in **joint ventures**—liquidity and pooling differ from simple consolidated cash
