## Executive Summary

**BLUF: Parker-Hannifin operates in attractive end markets with strong secular tailwinds, particularly aerospace (8%+ growth) and electrification. The company's transformed portfolio (2/3 longer-cycle/secular) reduces cyclical exposure while capturing growth in aerospace aftermarket, electrification, and infrastructure. Current market positioning is strong with record $11B backlog ($7.4B aerospace) providing visibility. Industrial markets showing gradual recovery with transportation challenges. Market share gains in aerospace and industrial aftermarket through distribution network. BUY - Well-positioned in secular growth markets with diversified exposure reducing cycle risk.**

## Aerospace & Defense Market

### Market Dynamics

**Market Size & Growth:**
- Global commercial aerospace market recovering strongly
- Defense spending increasing globally
- Aftermarket growth outpacing OEM growth
- Wide-body aircraft production ramping

**Parker's Position:**
- Record sales: $6.2B in FY25 (+13% organic)
- Record backlog: $7.4B (over 100% coverage)
- 51% aftermarket / 49% OEM mix (balanced)
- Proprietary designs on premier programs

### Growth Drivers

**Commercial Aerospace:**
- Air traffic recovery driving aftermarket demand
- Wide-body rates increasing to meet international traffic
- Commercial OEM: Low double-digit growth expected
- Commercial MRO: High single-digit growth expected

**Defense:**
- Department of Defense budget growth
- Legacy program demand continuing
- Defense OEM: Mid single-digit growth expected
- Defense MRO: Mid single-digit growth expected

**Competitive Advantages:**
- Global footprint supporting diverse customer base
- Long lifecycle programs with strong aftermarket
- More electric technologies for current & next-gen aircraft
- Proprietary designs on premier programs

### Market Outlook

**FY26 Expectations:**
- Overall aerospace: ~8% organic growth
- Higher growth in commercial OEM than commercial aftermarket
- Consistent growth throughout the year
- Each quarter expected to be record for aerospace

**Long-Term Trends:**
- Multi-year aerospace upcycle underway
- Fleet age supporting aftermarket growth
- New program ramps (737 MAX, A320neo, wide-body recovery)
- Defense modernization programs

## Industrial Markets

**Market Dynamics:**

- Assumes gradual industrial recovery
- Distributor sentiment positive
- MRO activity increasing

**Key Factors:**
- Customer supply chain actions benefiting MRO
- Factory retooling and refurbishment activity
- High quoting activity but some project delays
- Benefit from increased MRO activity

**Parker's Position:**
- Extensive global distribution network
- Serving small to mid-sized OEMs
- Industrial aftermarket strength
- Interconnected technologies advantage

### Transportation Market

**Market Challenges:**
- Most challenged market in FY25
- Mid-single digit organic decline expected in FY26
- Near-term pressure in auto and truck markets

**Challenges:**

- Uncertainty on cost and timing
- New emission requirements
- Current interest rate levels

**Market Segments:**
- **Automotive:** Weak demand, EV transition uncertainty
- **Truck:** Similar pressures, emission compliance costs
- **Rail:** More stable, infrastructure support

### HVAC/R Market

## Conclusion

Parker-Hannifin operates in attractive end markets with strong positioning in aerospace (growing ~8%) and industrial aftermarket. The company's portfolio transformation toward longer-cycle/secular revenue mix (2/3 currently, targeting 85%) reduces cyclical exposure while capturing growth in aerospace, electrification, and infrastructure. Record backlog ($11B, $7.4B aerospace) provides excellent visibility. Industrial markets are showing gradual recovery with transportation challenges. Market share gains through distribution network and technology leadership support continued outperformance. The company is well-positioned for secular growth trends while managing cyclical risks.
