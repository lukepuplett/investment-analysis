# Cloudflare, Inc. (NET) – Financial Analysis
**Date:** 04/29/2026 | **Period Analyzed:** Q4 FY2025

---

## Executive Summary

Cloudflare demonstrates strong revenue growth (30% CAGR since 2022) and improving unit economics. The company has achieved positive EBITDA ($105.8M, 4.88% margin) and substantial free cash flow ($388M TTM) despite GAAP losses. The path to profitability is clear: modest operating expense management could achieve GAAP breakeven within 12-18 months. Enterprise upsell (42% large customer growth) and pricing power support margin expansion. Risk is execution and whether growth can sustain above 30% CAGR.

**Financial Verdict:** Improving financial health with clear path to profitability. Valuation at 34x sales is justified by growth rate and cash generation, but leaves no margin for execution error.

## Profitability Analysis

### Path to Profitability

| Metric | TTM | 2024 | 2023 | 2022 | Trend |
|--------|-----|------|------|------|-------|
| **Revenue** | $2,167M | $1,670M | $1,297M | $975M | ↗ Growing |
| **Gross Profit** | $1,615M | $1,291M | $990M | $743M | ↗ Growing faster |
| **Gross Margin** | 74.5% | 77.3% | 76.4% | 76.1% | ↘ Slight compression |
| **Operating Loss** | ($207M) | ($155M) | ($185M) | ($201M) | ↗ **Improving** |
| **Operating Margin** | -9.6% | -9.3% | -14.3% | -20.6% | ↗ **Path clear** |
| **EBITDA** | $105.8M | $62M | ($36M) | ($83M) | ↗ **Inflection point** |
| **EBITDA Margin** | 4.88% | 3.7% | (2.8%) | (8.5%) | ↗ **Positive trend** |
| **Net Loss** | ($102M) | ($79M) | ($184M) | ($193M) | ↗ **Improving** |
| **Net Margin** | -4.7% | -4.7% | -14.2% | -19.8% | ↗ **Converging** |

### Key Findings

**1. EBITDA Inflection (2023 → 2025)**
- 2023: Negative EBITDA (-$36M)
- 2024: Positive EBITDA ($62M)
- 2025: Strong EBITDA ($106M, 4.88% margin)
- **Implication:** Operating model is maturing; positive operating leverage evident

**2. Operating Loss Improving Despite Continued Unprofitability**
- Operating loss has shrunk 50% from $201M (2022) → $207M (TTM)
- At current trajectory, operating income could reach breakeven in 12-18 months
- Only obstacle: continued aggressive investment in R&D and Sales

**3. Net Income Pressure from Interest/Non-Operating Items**
- Operating loss: ($207M)
- Interest income on cash: $131M (offset losses)
- Cash position ($4.1B) earning ~3% yields = ~$123M annual benefit
- **Implication:** Profitability path requires either: a) Operating leverage, or b) Deploying cash

## Margin Analysis

### Gross Margin Trends

| Metric | TTM | 2024 | 2023 | 2022 | Change |
|--------|-----|------|------|------|--------|
| **Gross Margin** | 74.5% | 77.3% | 76.4% | 76.1% | -2.8 pts |

**Why Compression?**
- Mix shift to Workers/Developer Platform (lower margin than CDN/Security)
- Increased paid customer traffic vs. free tier = more COGS allocation
- CFO commentary: "Underlying economics unchanged; just mix impact"

**Outlook:**
- Gross margin should stabilize at 74-75% (mid-term target: 75-77%)
- If Workers scale efficiently, margins could improve back to 76%+

### Operating Expense Ratio

| Category | TTM | 2024 | 2023 | 2022 | Trend |
|----------|-----|------|------|------|-------|
| **S&A / Revenue** | 60.4% | 61.3% | 63.1% | 66.2% | ↘ **Improving** |
| **R&D / Revenue** | 23.7% | 25.2% | 27.6% | 30.6% | ↘ **Improving** |
| **G&A / Revenue** | 18.0% | 16.6% | 16.8% | 18.4% | → **Stable** |
| **Total OpEx / Revenue** | 84.1% | 86.6% | 90.7% | 96.8% | ↘ **2.5 pts/year improvement** |

**Key Insight:** Operating expense ratio has improved 12.7 points since 2022 (from 96.8% → 84.1%). At this pace of improvement, Cloudflare reaches operating breakeven when operating expense ratio = gross margin (74.5%).

**Path to Breakeven:**
- Current OpEx/Revenue: 84.1%
- Gross Margin: 74.5%
- Gap: 9.6 points
- Pace of improvement: 2.5 points/year
- **Timeline:** 3-4 years at current pace, OR 12-18 months if growth accelerates and OpEx slows

## Financial Risks

### 1. **Growth Deceleration Risk**

**Risk:** If growth slows to <20% CAGR, profitability timeline extends significantly

**Evidence:**
- Currently 30% growth; trend is accelerating
- But hyperscaler competition could slow adoption

**Mitigation:**
- Multiple growth drivers (multi-cloud, Zero Trust, AI)
- Large customer segment growing 42% YoY

**Verdict:** Medium risk, but well-mitigated

---

### 2. **Margin Pressure Risk**

**Risk:** Gross margin compression could continue if Workers adoption outpaces higher-margin products

**Evidence:**
- Gross margin declined 2.8 points (77.3% → 74.5%)
- CFO noted "mix shift" to lower-margin products

**Mitigation:**
- Workers are strategic; margin optimization secondary
- Scale will improve unit economics over time

**Verdict:** Low risk. Margin compression is intentional, not concerning.

---

### 3. **Operating Expense Control Risk**

**Risk:** If company can't control OpEx growth, path to profitability extends

**Evidence Against:**
- OpEx/Revenue has improved 12.7 points since 2022
- Each segment improving (S&A, R&D, G&A all declining as % of revenue)

**Verdict:** Low risk. Management has demonstrated discipline.

---

### 4. **Debt Service Risk**

**Risk:** $3.5B debt load could become burdensome if growth slows

**Assessment:**
- Interest coverage is strong (15.6x)
- FCF can easily cover interest
- Debt maturity profile matters (not disclosed)

**Verdict:** Low risk given FCF generation.
