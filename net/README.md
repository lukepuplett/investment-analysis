## Company Overview

**Founded:** 2009 by Matthew Prince and Michelle Zatlyn

**Business Model:**  
Cloudflare provides a unified platform for connecting, protecting, and building the internet. The company operates across three main segments:
- **Application Services** — Content delivery, bot management, DDoS protection, WAF
- **Developer Platform** — Cloudflare Workers (serverless computing), Pages, D1 database
- **Zero Trust** — Identity and access management, secure web gateway, SASE

**Geographic Presence:** Global, with major presence in US, Europe, APAC

**Customer Base:** 4,298 large customers (>$100K/year annual spend, up 23% YoY), representing 73% of revenue

## Strategic Positioning

### Competitive Advantages
1. **Unified Platform** — All-in-one solution vs. best-of-breed alternatives (reduces buyer friction)
2. **Network Effects** — Global network of data centers provides performance/security advantages
3. **Developer Mindset** — Strong reputation with engineering teams; product-led growth motion
4. **Brand & Trust** — Recognized as independent alternative to hyperscalers (AWS, Azure, GCP)

### Growth Drivers
1. **AI/ML Infrastructure** — Workers AI and Cloudflare's network for LLM inference
2. **Security Consolidation** — Market shift from point products to integrated platforms (SASE)
3. **Developer Platform Adoption** — Workers gaining traction as serverless alternative
4. **Enterprise Upsell** — 4,298 large customers show migration upmarket

### Key Risks
1. **Hyperscaler Competition** — AWS, Azure, GCP have larger resource base and existing customer relationships
2. **Profitability Path** — Currently unprofitable; must demonstrate GAAP earnings within 12-18 months
3. **Growth Sustainability** — Can 30%+ growth continue as company scales to $2B+ revenue?
4. **Customer Concentration** — Largest customer likely <5% but still a single-point-of-failure risk
5. **Valuation** — 34x sales leaves little margin for error if growth slows
