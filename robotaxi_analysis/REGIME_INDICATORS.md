## Leading Indicator Hierarchy

Monitor in this order — earlier tiers have more predictive power.

**Tier 1: Ownership Substitution %** (most important)  
Unlocks all other variables. If true, take-rates stay high, approval accelerates, fleet scales. If false, margins compress, approval bureaucratic.

- <5% all metros by Q4 2027 → Confirm A
- 15–30% major metros by Q4 2027 → Confirm B  
- 35–50% in 2+ metros by Q4 2028 → C likely

**Tier 2: Regulatory Approval Pace**  
Must align with autonomy progress. Faster than ownership change = narrative mismatch.

- 2–6%/year sustained → Confirm A
- Accelerating to 5–8%/year by 2027 → Confirm B
- >10%/year by 2028 → C signal

**Tier 3: Non-Ride Revenue Mix**  
Reveals if asset layer is monetizing. Regime C requires 30%+ of miles non-ride.

- <5% of miles by Q4 2027 → Confirm A
- 10–25% emerging by Q4 2027 → Confirm B
- >30% of miles by Q4 2028 → C signal

**Tier 4: Take-Rate Trajectory**  
Derivative of ownership + competition. If ownership replacing cars, rates stay 35%+. If not, Uber re-enters.

- 18–32% stable with Uber/Lyft pressure → Confirm A
- 25–40% sustainable without demand erosion → Confirm B
- 40–65% resilient despite fleet scaling → C signal

**Tier 5: Fleet Guidance**  
Reveals internal TAM model. Secondary to tiers 1–4.

- <300k fleet target → A
- 500k–2M fleet target → B
- >5M fleet target → C

---

## Decision Rules

Need 3–4 Tier 1–2 signals to force regime shift. Do not wait for all to align — if any hard trigger hits, the regime is dead.

**Data sources by metric:**
- Ownership substitution: Census vehicle ownership, insurance dropoffs, rideshare surveys
- Approval pace: Regulatory filings, operator metro announcements per quarter
- Non-ride mix: Operator press releases, fleet telemetry analysis
- Take-rate: Earnings reports, gross revenue ÷ miles
- Fleet guidance: Earnings calls, capex plans, investor presentations
