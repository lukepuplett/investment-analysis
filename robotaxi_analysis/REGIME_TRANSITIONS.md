# Regime Transitions: Observable Triggers

Regime shifts are not gradual—they're discontinuous. Hard triggers force transitions when data contradicts regime assumptions. Soft signals precede hard triggers.

---

## Regime A → B Transition

**Preconditions for Shift:** One assumption breaking signals regime drift. **Hard trigger** forces acceptance of regime B model.

### Soft Signals (Watch for Trend)

| Signal | Regime A Baseline | Regime B Threshold | Monitoring |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|------------|
| Ownership substitution trend | <5% flat | 5–10% and accelerating | Quarterly (survey, rideshare data) |
| Take-rate trajectory | 18–32% stable | >25% sustained despite competition | Quarterly (operator reports, pricing) |
| Non-ride use cases | <5% of miles | 5–10% of miles (emerging signals) | Quarterly (fleet telemetry) |
| Approval velocity | 3–6%/year | >6%/year trending up | Quarterly (regulatory filings) |
| Fleet growth guidance | <300k cumulative | >300k→500k guidance shift | Annual (earnings calls) |
| Competitive dynamics | Both players scaling equally | Waymo outpacing Tesla or vice versa | Semi-annual (fleet announcements) |

### Hard Trigger (Forces Regime B Adoption)

**Any ONE of these becomes observable fact:**

1. **Ownership substitution >10%** in major deployment metro (SF Bay, Phoenix, Austin, LA)
   - Definition: % of households without personal vehicle ownership, adopting robotaxi as primary transport
   - Data source: Census data, local surveys, insurance industry reports
   - Threshold: 10% confirmed in 1+ metro = regime cannot sustain A economics
   - Implication: Take-rates will rise, fleet will expand, approval will accelerate

2. **Take-rate holds >30%** for 2+ consecutive quarters without demand erosion
   - Definition: Revenue per mile at 30%+ despite competition from Uber/Lyft (if operating in same market)
   - Data source: Operator earnings reports, pricing transparency
   - Threshold: >30% confirmed for 2+ quarters = competition not persisting as Regime A assumes
   - Implication: Ownership replacement dynamics emerging; margin expansion possible

3. **Non-ride revenue >10% of platform miles** (delivery, logistics, ads, subsidized transport)
   - Definition: % of robotaxi fleet miles deployed on non-ride use cases
   - Data source: Fleet telemetry, operator breakdowns
   - Threshold: >10% of miles = asset-layer monetization emerging (not pure ride-hailing)
   - Implication: Platform dynamics beginning; asset leverage expanding

4. **Regulatory approval >8%/year sustained** for 2+ consecutive years
   - Definition: Geographic expansion of robotaxi operations (% of population served)
   - Data source: Regulatory filings, operator announcements
   - Threshold: >8%/year = regulatory environment fundamentally changed from bureaucratic/linear
   - Implication: Safety narrative shifting; approval inflection starting

5. **Fleet guidance or deployment >500k vehicles cumulative**
   - Definition: Total operating + announced fleet size across all operators
   - Data source: Company guidance, fleet announcements
   - Threshold: >500k committed = TAM shift from 5% rideshare to ownership replacement
   - Implication: Economics require 20%+ ownership substitution to justify deployment

### Timeline to Hard Trigger
- **Expected:** By Q4 2027 (if Regime B likely)
- **Outside window:** After Q4 2028 without trigger = confirm Regime A (or collapse to A if tried scaling)

## Regime B → C Transition

**Preconditions for Shift:** Multiple assumptions breaking simultaneously signals platform dynamics. **Hard trigger** forces Regime C adoption.

### Soft Signals (Watch for Trend)

| Signal | Regime B Baseline | Regime C Threshold | Monitoring |
|--------|-------------------|-------------------|------------|
| Ownership substitution trend | 15–30% growing | >30% and accelerating | Quarterly |
| Take-rate trajectory | 25–40% stable | >40% despite fleet scale | Quarterly |
| Non-ride use cases | 10–25% of miles | >25% of miles (platform layer growing) | Quarterly |
| Approval velocity | 5–8%/year S-curve | >10%/year (exponential, not S-curve) | Quarterly |
| Fleet growth guidance | 500k–2M | >2M guidance shift | Annual |
| Marginal cost decline | 20–30%/year | >30%/year (software scaling evident) | Semi-annual (analysis) |
| Competitive concentration | <60% to any player | >60% to leader, <40% to second | Semi-annual |

### Hard Trigger (Forces Regime C Adoption)

**Any ONE of these becomes observable fact:**

1. **Ownership substitution >30%** in major metro AND trending toward 40%+
   - Definition: >30% of households primary transport is robotaxi
   - Data source: Census, local surveys, insurance dropoff, public transit data
   - Threshold: >30% confirmed in 1+ major metro = ownership replacement is structural
   - Implication: Ride demand goes exponential; take-rates sustain 40%+; fleet scales nonlinearly

2. **Regulatory approval >10%/year sustained** for 2+ consecutive years (exponential, not S-curve)
   - Definition: Geographic expansion accelerating (e.g., 10% of population served per year)
   - Data source: Regulatory filings
   - Threshold: >10%/year confirmed = regulatory inflection to "human driving dangerous" narrative
   - Implication: Exponential approval path; timeline aligns with 50%+ ownership replacement by 2035

3. **Non-ride revenue >30% of platform miles** (network effects, ads, logistics dominating)
   - Definition: >30% of fleet miles non-ride use cases
   - Data source: Fleet telemetry
   - Threshold: >30% = platform layer exceeds core ride revenue; computing dynamics
   - Implication: Valuation inflects to software multiples (50–150x); margins sustain 40%+

4. **One player consolidates >75% in any tier** (metro, regional, or national)
   - Definition: Winner captures >75% of deployment; second player <25%
   - Data source: Market share data, fleet deployment announcements
   - Threshold: >75% confirmed = software scaling compounding; winner-take-most dynamics
   - Implication: Competitive moat insurmountable; platform monopoly emerging

5. **Marginal cost decline >30%/year sustained** (software dynamics, not hardware plateau)
   - Definition: Cost per robotaxi deployment declining 30%+ annually
   - Data source: Operator capex guidance, analyst models
   - Threshold: >30%/year confirmed = autonomy stack generalizing; scaling exponential
   - Implication: Leader can undercut competition; cost curve enables 10M+ fleet profitably

6. **Fleet guidance or deployment >5M vehicles cumulative** with increasing utilization per vehicle
   - Definition: Total operating fleet reaches 5M+
   - Data source: Company guidance, fleet announcements
   - Threshold: >5M = TAM shift to ownership replacement complete; fleet size implies 50%+ ownership substitution
   - Implication: Software scaling evident; Regime C dynamics in full force

### Timeline to Hard Trigger
- **Expected:** By Q4 2028 (if Regime C likely)
- **Outside window:** After Q4 2030 without trigger = confirm Regime B (or demote to A if approval stalls)

## Regime B → A Collapse (Fallback)

**Hard Triggers (Forces Regime A if ANY true):**

1. **Ownership substitution <10%** despite 3+ years wide availability
   - Implication: Ownership replacement not materializing; robotaxis remain niche

2. **Approval stays <2%/year** sustained for 2+ consecutive years
   - Implication: Regulatory environment remains bureaucratic; not civilization-scale shift

3. **Take-rate compresses <18%** (competition re-emerges at Regime A levels)
   - Implication: Uber/Lyft reenter market at scale; margins collapse to transport commodity

4. **Intervention rate plateaus** flat for 4+ consecutive quarters
   - Implication: Autonomy not improving; fragility evident; safety narrative fails

5. **Fleet guidance <500k** after 5+ years of deployment
   - Implication: Demand insufficient for ownership replacement model

### Timeline to Fallback
- **Critical window:** Q4 2027–Q2 2028
- **If no progress:** Accept Regime A, model collapses in valuation

---

## Regime C → B Degradation (Downshift)

**Hard Triggers (Forces Regime B if ANY true):**

1. **Autonomy stalls** (intervention rate flat 4+ quarters despite continued tuning)
   - Implication: Stack doesn't generalize; region-specific tuning forever; not software scaling

2. **Second player matches leader growth rate** (closes gap from 70% to 50/50 or narrower)
   - Implication: Software scaling not compounding; bifurcated outcome, not monopoly

3. **Approval inflection fails** (stays <8%/year; S-curve doesn't accelerate to exponential)
   - Implication: Regulatory environment S-curves as in Regime B; no exponential flip

4. **Ownership growth plateaus <40%** after 5+ years of deployment
   - Implication: Ownership replacement stops shy of platform threshold

5. **Take-rate compresses <35%** despite large fleet despite fleet scale and non-ride revenue
   - Implication: Competitive dynamics re-emerge; margins don't support platform economics

### Timeline to Downshift
- **Trigger assessment:** Annual (Q4 2027 through 2030)
- **If triggered:** Degrade to Regime B economics; valuation resets

---

## Regime C → A Collapse (Catastrophic)

**Hard Triggers (Forces Regime A if ANY true):**

1. **Major autonomy failure or public incident** (fatality, multi-vehicle crash, regulatory backlash)
   - Implication: Safety narrative reversed; approval collapses; ownership substitution reverses

2. **Regulatory approval reversed** (pilot shut down, expansion halted)
   - Implication: Regulatory environment flip from pro to cautious

3. **Ownership substitution reversed** after earlier growth (e.g., drops from 35% back to <10%)
   - Implication: Customer loss of confidence; robotaxis re-stigmatized

### Timeline to Collapse
- **Immediate:** Upon triggering event
- **Valuation impact:** Catastrophic reset to Regime A (90%+ loss relative to Regime C midpoint)
