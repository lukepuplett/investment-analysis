## Executive Summary

**BLUF: RYCEY trades at £16.38, implying 17.2x P/E (2025E), 12.0x EV/EBITDA, 4.7x P/S, and 2.7% FCF yield. Valuation is reasonable-to-attractive vs. peers (aerospace industry avg 23x P/E, 14x EV/EBITDA) given above-peer margins and growth. DCF analysis suggests fair value £20–26 per share (base case £23–24), implying 40–50% upside. Sum-of-parts analysis corroborates similar range. Valuation supported by expanding margin profile (20.8% → 23% by 2028), robust FCF generation (£3.6B, 16.9% margin), and defensive LTSA recurring revenue base. Downside protected by net cash position and aftermarket stickiness. Valuation best justifies long-term (3–5 year) buy-and-hold strategy; short-term trading risk elevated due to cyclicality.**

## IV. Sum-of-Parts (SOTP) Valuation

### Segment Valuation Approach

**Assumption:** Value each business segment separately, then sum to enterprise value.

#### Civil Aerospace Segment (~60% of revenue, £12.7B)

**Characteristics:**
- LTSA recurring revenue (40–50% of segment)
- High margin (20–22% operating margin)
- Widebody dominant (A350 monopoly)
- Growth driver (UltraFan potential)

**Valuation:**
- LTSA Revenue: £5.1B recurring
- Recurring FCF: £1.2B (LTSA margin 23%)
- Terminal value (3% perpetual growth): £1.2B × 1.03 / 5% = £24.7B
- Widebody/Narrowbody upside: +£8B (UltraFan success option)
- Segment Enterprise Value: £32.7B

#### Defense Segment (~10% of revenue, £2.1B)

**Characteristics:**
- Government contracts (sticky, recurring)
- Stable margins (14–16%)
- Low growth (3–5%)

**Valuation:**
- Operating profit: £0.3B (15% margin)
- Terminal value: £0.3B × 1.02 / 5% = £6.1B
- Defense Premium (strategic value): +£1B
- Segment Enterprise Value: £7.1B

#### Power Systems Segment (~15% of revenue, £3.2B)

**Characteristics:**
- SMR pre-commercial (high risk, option value)
- Gas turbine base (mature)
- Strategic importance (energy transition)

**Valuation:**
- Current Operating profit: £0.4B (12% margin)
- Mature baseload: £4B
- SMR Option Value (prob 40%, 2035+ commercialization): £2–4B
- Segment Enterprise Value: £6–8B
