# Rolls-Royce Holdings plc (RYCEY) – Financial Analysis
**Analysis Date:** December 31, 2025

---

## Executive Summary

**BLUF: Rolls-Royce has undergone a dramatic financial transformation (2022–2025), swinging from distressed loss of £1.3B to profit of £5.8B, with operating margins improving 3.4x to 21.1% and free cash flow tripling to £3.6B. The business has returned to positive equity (£2.7B), reduced debt by 28%, and built a net cash position. The transformation reflects operational improvements (higher LTSA margins, aftermarket growth, power systems restructuring) validated by strong cash generation and disciplined capital allocation. Key risks remain execution on midterm targets, supply chain sustainability, and leverage ratios, but the financial trajectory is decisively positive.**

## Conclusion

Rolls-Royce's financial transformation from 2022–2025 is one of the most compelling industrial turnarounds in recent memory. The company has moved from distressed (negative equity, high leverage, losses) to a position of financial strength (positive equity, net cash, strong profitability, exceptional cash generation). 

**The financial story is credible because:**
- Revenue growth is organic and broad-based across three divisions
- Margin expansion is driven by structural improvements (LTSA, aftermarket, efficiency) not accounting tricks
- Cash conversion is exceptional (FCF £3.6B supports all prior claims)
- Balance sheet transformation is real (positive equity, debt reduction, net cash position)
- Capital allocation reflects disciplined management (balanced debt reduction and shareholder returns)

**Financial risks are manageable** because net leverage is low (~0.5x), FCF generation is robust, and the company has room to absorb operational hiccups while maintaining dividend and progressing buybacks.

For a company at this inflection point, the financial metrics support a constructive medium-term thesis, contingent on execution of midterm targets and supply chain stability.
