## Cash Flow Analysis & Key Insights

### Operating Performance
- **Strong OCF Growth:** Operating cash flow nearly tripled from £1.5B (2022) to £4.6B (2025)
- **Improving Quality:** OCF/Revenue ratio improved from 11.3% to 21.5% — indicating higher quality earnings
- **Cash Conversion:** 78.1% cash conversion on net income (healthy for industrial business)

### Capital Investment
- **Disciplined CapEx:** Capital expenditure represents 4.6% of revenue (stable and moderate)
- **Consistent Growth Capex:** Real capex growing as business scales but not excessive

### Free Cash Flow
- **FCF Tripled:** From £928M (2022) to £3.6B (2025) — 286% growth
- **FCF Margin Expansion:** Grew from 6.9% to 16.9% of revenue
- **Credibility Signal:** Strong FCF generation validates operational turnaround thesis

### Debt Management
- **Aggressive Paydown:** Repaid £927M in 2025 alone (vs £2.0B one-time repayment in 2022)
- **Debt Reduction Trajectory:** Total debt declining steadily; FCF now covers debt repayment 4.9x
- **De-Leveraging:** Focus on debt reduction while also returning capital to shareholders

### Capital Allocation Shift
- **2022-2024:** Debt repayment priority (£2.0B+ repaid in 2022, £475M in 2024)
- **2025:** More balanced approach — £927M debt repayment + £1.0B share buyback (announced £7-9B buyback for 2026-2028)
- **Confidence Signal:** Shift to buybacks reflects management confidence in sustainable cash generation

### Cash Position
- **Strong Liquidity:** Cash balance up 140% (£2.6B → £6.2B)
- **Net Cash Position:** Now holds net cash (£6.2B cash vs £4.3B debt)
- **War Chest Ready:** Sufficient cash for strategic optionality (M&A, R&D investment, etc.)
