## Executive Summary (BLUF - Bottom Line Up Front)

**Rating: BUY** | **Target Price (2028E): £22–24** | **Current Price: £16.38** | **Upside: +34–47%**

Rolls-Royce is a compelling deep-value operational turnaround story in the aerospace/defense sector. The company has executed a financial transformation (2022–2025) swinging from distressed equity (£6B deficit) to profitability (£5.8B earnings), with operating margins improving 1,500 bps to 20.8% and free cash flow tripling to £3.6B. Supply-constrained commercial aviation demand through 2027 provides a near-term tailwind; long-term service agreement (LTSA) recurring revenue provides defensive base with structural margin expansion potential. UltraFan narrowbody engine represents credible 15–20% market share opportunity worth £2–3B incremental revenue by 2030. Financial metrics are attractive: valuation 23% discount to peers on P/E (17.2x vs. 22.4x), fortress balance sheet (net cash £1.8B), and strong cash generation (2.7% FCF yield, above-peer). Key execution risks are UltraFan certification and supply chain sustainability, but not existential given aftermarket stickiness and net cash position. Recommended for growth portfolios with 3–5 year time horizon.

## Company Overview

**Rolls-Royce Holdings plc** is a British multinational aerospace and defense company with three core business segments:

1. **Civil Aerospace (~60% revenue):** Commercial jet engines (Trent widebody platform dominant on Airbus A350, co-supplier Boeing 787), long-term service agreements (LTSA/aftermarket, recurring revenue base), narrowbody engines (entering with UltraFan)

2. **Defense & Security (~10% revenue):** Combat propulsion (Eurofighter Typhoon EJ200, 30% consortium share), submarine propulsion (UK Astute, French Barracuda), transport/logistics support (RAF, NATO)

3. **Power Systems (~15% revenue):** Small modular reactors (SMR, pre-commercial), gas turbine power generation, battery storage (emerging)

**Headquarters:** Derby, UK  
**Key Plants:** Derby (HQ), Singapore, North Carolina (USA)  
**Employees:** ~50,000+  
**Listing:** London Stock Exchange (LSE: RR.); ADR (RYCEY)

## Investment Thesis Summary

### Five Core Pillars (Confidence Levels)

1. **Operational Turnaround (Proven)** — 9/10
   - Financial restructuring complete (distressed equity → profitability)
   - Multiple segments contributing to profit growth (all divisions +)
   - Sustainable cash generation validated (FCF £3.6B, margin 16.9%)

2. **Supply-Constrained Aerospace Demand (2025–2027 Tailwind)** — 8/10
   - Commercial aviation capacity shortage (demand >supply through 2027)
   - Narrowbody engines 2,400+/year demand; 1,800/year supply
   - Order backlog 5–8 years (revenue visibility)
   - Premium pricing environment supports margins

3. **LTSA Recurring Revenue Base (Defensive, Growing)** — 9/10
   - 30–50 year locked-in maintenance contracts (customer switching costs high)
   - Margin expansion 19 pp contract path; 28 pp income statement flow (2022–2028)
   - Aftermarket 40–50% of civil aero revenue (sticky, non-discretionary)
   - Enables cycle-resistive cash generation

4. **UltraFan Narrowbody Opportunity (High-Risk, High-Reward)** — 6/10
   - Addresses £30B+ narrowbody market (RYCEY currently 15–20% share)
   - Technical differentiation clear (-25% fuel burn vs. CFM56 baseline)
   - Demonstrator underway; OEM certification 2026–2027 target
   - Success = 15–20% market share, £2–3B revenue opportunity by 2030
   - Execution risk real; certification delays (40% probability), adoption uncertainty

5. **Defense & Optionality (Strategic Long-Term)** — 7/10
   - Defense budgets rising (geopolitical tailwinds, NATO expansion)
   - Submarine programs multi-decade contracts (strategic moat)
   - SMR technology represents £50B+ market optionality (pre-commercial 2030+)
   - Non-core near-term but valuable option value
