## Question 3: Price-to-Book Ratio Explanation

**Date:** October 21, 2025  
**Topic:** Price-to-Book Ratio Analysis  
**Question:** Explain price-to-book ratio and what it means for SPCE

### Answer

The Price-to-Book (P/B) ratio is a fundamental valuation metric that compares a company's market value to its book value (net asset value). For Virgin Galactic, this ratio provides important insights into market sentiment and potential undervaluation.

#### Price-to-Book Ratio Formula
```
P/B Ratio = Market Price per Share ÷ Book Value per Share
P/B Ratio = Market Cap ÷ Total Shareholders' Equity
```

#### SPCE Price-to-Book Analysis
| Metric | Value | Calculation |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| **Market Cap** | $232.67M | Current market value |
| **Total Shareholders' Equity** | $322.28M | From balance sheet |
| **P/B Ratio** | **0.88x** | $232.67M ÷ $322.28M |
| **Book Value per Share** | $6.78 | $322.28M ÷ 47.6M shares |
| **Market Price** | $3.91 | Current stock price |

#### What P/B Ratio Means

##### P/B < 1.0 (SPCE = 0.88x)
- **Trading Below Book Value:** Market values company at less than its net assets
- **Potential Undervaluation:** Assets worth more than market cap
- **Asset Backstop:** Shareholders' equity provides downside protection
- **Market Pessimism:** Investors discounting future prospects

##### P/B > 1.0 (Typical Range)
- **Trading Above Book Value:** Market values company above net assets
- **Growth Premium:** Investors paying for future earnings potential
- **Intangible Value:** Brand, technology, customer relationships valued
- **Market Optimism:** Positive outlook on future performance

#### SPCE P/B Analysis

##### Current Situation (P/B = 0.88x)
| Factor | Impact | Analysis |
|--------|--------|----------|
| **Asset Protection** | Positive | $322M equity vs $233M market cap |
| **Undervaluation Signal** | Positive | Trading below asset value |
| **Market Sentiment** | Negative | Investors discounting prospects |
| **Risk Premium** | High | Significant execution risk priced in |

##### Historical Context
| Period | P/B Ratio | Market Sentiment |

#### Investment Implications
