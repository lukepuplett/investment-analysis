# DoorDash Inc. (DASH) - Q3 2025 Earnings Press Release

**Date:** November 5, 2025  
**Quarter:** Q3 2025 (Ended September 30, 2025)  
**Source:** DoorDash Investor Relations

---

## Executive Summary

DoorDash reported strong Q3 2025 results with accelerated year-over-year growth in revenue, Total Orders, and Marketplace GOV. The company generated nearly $24 billion in combined sales for merchants and earnings for Dashers in Q3 2025, and expects to generate well over $100 billion in 2026.

**Key Highlights:**
- Total Orders increased 21% Y/Y to 776 million
- Marketplace GOV increased 25% Y/Y to $25.0 billion
- Revenue increased 27% Y/Y to $3.4 billion
- GAAP net income increased 51% Y/Y to $244 million
- Adjusted EBITDA increased 41% Y/Y to $754 million

## Operational Highlights

### Growth Drivers
- **Marketplace GOV growth accelerated** for second consecutive quarter
- Growth driven primarily by **strong growth in monthly active users (MAUs)**
- Contributions from Y/Y growth in average order frequency and average order values
- Currency impact: less than 1% to Y/Y growth in Marketplace GOV

### U.S. Marketplace Performance
- **Strong growth in U.S. MAUs** through first nine months of 2025
- Added nearly **twice as many MAUs** in first 9M 2025 vs first 9M 2024
- **U.S. DashPass membership** exceeded full-year expectations for paid member additions
- **U.S. restaurant category:** Y/Y growth accelerated to highest rate in more than three years
- Unit economics in U.S. restaurant category increased Y/Y and were consistent with Q2 2025

### U.S. New Verticals
- Expanded selection in Q3 2025, including key new partnerships in grocery and retail
- Launched **DashMart Fulfillment Services** - infrastructure for consistent, higher-quality consumer experiences
- Consumer engagement continued to grow:
  - Q/Q and Y/Y increases in MAUs ordering from new verticals
  - Q/Q and Y/Y increases in average order frequency
  - Q/Q and Y/Y increases in average order values
- **Accelerated Y/Y growth** in Marketplace GOV from U.S. new verticals
- **Improved unit economics** Y/Y and Q/Q (though still negative)
- **Reduced average consumer transaction fees** Y/Y and Q/Q

### International Marketplaces
- **Unit economics reached new all-time high** in Q3 2025
- Continued investment in products and consumer acquisition
- **Y/Y growth in international MAUs accelerated slightly** in Q3 2025
- Y/Y growth in Total Orders decelerated slightly due to slower Y/Y growth in average order frequency
- **Y/Y growth in Marketplace GOV remained strong** in Q3 2025
- **Outgrew category peers** in majority of countries where third parties report transactional data

### Deliveroo Acquisition
- **Closed acquisition** on October 2, 2025
- **Equity value:** £2.8 billion, including estimated cash and short-term investments acquired of £690 million
- **Combined metrics** (DoorDash + Wolt + Deliveroo):
  - Over 50 million MAUs
  - Over 30 million consumers through membership programs
  - Partner with more than 1 million merchants
  - Over $100 billion in annualized Marketplace GOV
  - Across more than 40 countries

## Financial Performance Details

### Revenue
- Revenue increased 27% Y/Y to $3.4 billion in Q3 2025
- Primarily due to Y/Y increase in Marketplace GOV
- Net Revenue Margin: 13.8% (up from 13.5% in Q3 2024 and Q2 2025)
- Y/Y and Q/Q increases in Net Revenue Margin due to:
  - Increasing contribution from advertising revenue
  - Reduction in credits and refunds as a percentage of Marketplace GOV
  - Reduction in Dasher costs as a percentage of Marketplace GOV

### Cost of Revenue
- GAAP cost of revenue (exclusive of D&A): $1.7 billion in Q3 2025
- Up 23% Y/Y and up 4% Q/Q
- Driven primarily by increases in Total Orders
- As % of Marketplace GOV: 6.7% (down from 6.9% in Q3 2024, consistent with 6.7% in Q2 2025)

### Gross Profit
- GAAP gross profit: $1.7 billion in Q3 2025
- Up 32% Y/Y and 5% Q/Q
- GAAP gross profit as % of Marketplace GOV: 6.8% (up from 6.4% in Q3 2024 and 6.6% in Q2 2025)

### Operating Expenses

**Sales and Marketing:**
- $576 million in Q3 2025, up 19% Y/Y and down 5% Q/Q
- Y/Y increase driven by advertising expenses and personnel-related compensation
- Q/Q decrease driven by reduction in advertising expenses
- As % of Marketplace GOV: 2.3% (down from 2.4% in Q3 2024 and 2.5% in Q2 2025)

**Research and Development:**
- $355 million in Q3 2025, up 23% Y/Y and up 1% Q/Q
- Y/Y increase driven by personnel-related compensation expenses
- As % of Marketplace GOV: 1.4% (consistent with Q3 2024 and Q2 2025)

**General and Administrative:**
- $400 million in Q3 2025, up 27% Y/Y and up 3% Q/Q
- Y/Y increase driven by legal, tax, and regulatory expenses and transaction-related costs
- Q/Q increase driven by legal, tax, and regulatory expenses
- As % of Marketplace GOV: 1.6% (consistent with Q3 2024 and Q2 2025)

### Net Income
- GAAP net income: $244 million in Q3 2025
- Increase from $162 million in Q3 2024 (+51%)
- Decrease from $285 million in Q2 2025

### Adjusted EBITDA
- $754 million in Q3 2025
- Up 41% from $533 million in Q3 2024
- Up 15% from $655 million in Q2 2025
- Adjusted EBITDA as % of Marketplace GOV: 3.0% (up from 2.7% in Q3 2024 and 2.7% in Q2 2025)

### Cash Flow
- Net cash provided by operating activities: $871 million in Q3 2025 (up from $531 million in Q3 2024)
- Free Cash Flow: $723 million in Q3 2025 (up from $444 million in Q3 2024)

## Investment Philosophy

**Primary Financial Goal:** Maximize long-term free cash flow per share

**Two Primary Forms of Investment:**

1. **Drive operating efficiencies** in existing areas of business that can be invested back into product improvements to increase scale and extend duration

2. **Constantly invest in entirely new opportunities** where DoorDash believes it has the ideas, people, and capital to solve challenges for stakeholders

**Product Development:**
- More product development underway today than at any point in history
- Recent expansions:
  - DoorDash for Business
  - Drive
  - Online Ordering
- New initiatives:
  - DashMart Fulfillment Services
  - Going Out
  - In-Store rewards
  - Creator partnerships
- New tools:
  - New mapping platform
  - SmartScale
  - Autonomous Delivery Platform
  - Dot (customized autonomous delivery robot)

**Global Technology Platform:**
- Multi-year effort began in 2024
- Made meaningful progress in 2025
- Expected to accelerate in 2026
- Expected to:
  - Increase pace of global product development
  - Enhance developer productivity through AI-native tooling
  - Improve operational consistency across 40+ countries

**2026 Investment Plans:**
- Expect to invest **several hundred million dollars more** in new initiatives and platform development in 2026 than in 2025

## Definitions

### Key Metrics

**Total Orders:** All orders completed through Marketplaces and Commerce Platform over the period of measurement.

**Marketplace GOV:** Total dollar value of orders completed on Marketplaces, including taxes, tips, and any applicable consumer fees, including membership fees related to DashPass and Wolt+. Does not include dollar value of orders fulfilled through Commerce Platform.

**Net Revenue Margin:** Revenue expressed as a percentage of Marketplace GOV.

**Monthly Active Users (MAUs):** Number of individual consumer accounts that completed an order on marketplaces in the last month of the period of measurement.

**Average Order Frequency:** Total number of orders completed on marketplaces divided by the number of individual consumer accounts that completed an order on marketplaces in the period of measurement.

**Average Order Value:** Total value of Marketplace GOV divided by the total number of orders completed on marketplaces in the period of measurement.

**Members:** Number of member accounts for relevant membership programs on the last day of the period of measurement.

**Consumer Transaction Fees:** Total value of consumer fees excluding subscription fees, divided by total orders in the period of measurement.
